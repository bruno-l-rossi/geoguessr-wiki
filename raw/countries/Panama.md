### Step 1 - Identifying Panama

![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/panama/0_1.png)

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/panama/2.png)](https://www.plonkit.net/images/panama/2.png)

Panama features short licence plates, most commonly white with a horizontal **turquoise strip** on the **top**. Rarely, the strip can have [other colours](https://goo.gl/maps/xCKZgTN28Dzp9W8w6).

Front plates are **not** required in Panama.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/panama/3.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/wAUy9vFzP6jNjK31A)

Panama was covered by a large **white pickup truck** with a white metal frame behind the driver's cabin, that can either be fully visible or mostly blurred. There is always a short black antenna attached to the front of the car, which may sometimes also be [blurred](https://www.google.com/maps/@7.9966524,-80.4728265,3a,21.6y,91.11h,78.72t/data=!3m6!1e1!3m4!1sk6hV_LI76h6Y0FdbhcAEUQ!2e0!7i16384!8i8192?entry=ttu).

NOTE: The [Costa Rican](https://maps.app.goo.gl/468aLVF3daz21MRLA) truck does not have this white metal frame. Also, a fully blurred out car is more common in Panama. The car used in [Ecuador](https://maps.app.goo.gl/dSweUPGTkgTasq9B7) has neither an antenna nor a white metal railing.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/panama/4.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/mxghYG8jVb6Hgbvq5)

Panama is a tropical country with a lot of hills. You will find a lot of palms and other tropical plants along with drier rolling hills and fields.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/panama/5.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/oj7zt58pNoPgEp8K8)

Most roads are paralleled by fences that are partly or fully made out of small trees. The rest of the fence posts are also almost always wooden.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/panama/6.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/cEXFmgQg32UswRSG9)

Panamanian architecture often reflects **Spanish colonial influences**, featuring vibrant colours, and some ornate detailing. The most common type of building has **painted concrete** or sometimes brickwalls with red metal sheets or tiles for a roof. Manywindows have bars on them for security.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/panama/7.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/v5qnArZ7E531Q4v19)

Panama features a diverse set of road lines, but the most common combination is [solid outer white with an inner yellow line](https://goo.gl/maps/KAGUqGk3zVKLnNXH8). However, those are not unique to Panama. Within Latin America, solid outer white lines with no middle line are almost unique to Panama.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/panama/8.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/Lqdhmbem6HJvdX2Q7)

Panama uses a wide variety of poles, but the most common type is a **round concrete pole**, with a varying amount of insulators. Poles can also be wooden or metallic. They often have [plates](https://goo.gl/maps/5u2MsQ5wAd6cLHTt7) attached to them. [Blue](https://goo.gl/maps/wCUF3vx7iUtNgZjAA), [white](https://goo.gl/maps/4wmrWMk22ZnZtY3x5), or [red](https://goo.gl/maps/4tDyan7Qz2WnrPsX9) numbers are commonly painted on them as well.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/panama/9.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/eXwqy2CCBmp5iGJn8)

**Yellow** taxis with a **chequerboard patterned horizontal stripe** are a common sight in Panama. The licence plate will be drawn on the side, containing the city name and a [regional code](https://www.plonkit.net/panama#taxiplates).

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/panama/10.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/LaAjCE36HSZziiCg8)

Panama uses the Spanish word “ **ALTO** ” on stop signs.

NOTE: [Mexico](https://goo.gl/maps/f1zLdwAo5VhYDhtC7), [Costa Rica](https://maps.app.goo.gl/K6pZqyNn2Hw52GXJ7) and [Guatemala](https://www.google.com/maps/@14.6255005,-90.5568668,3a,15y,74.14h,81.77t/data=!3m6!1e1!3m4!1smSi97ivNOrtG5_baoEx9YQ!2e0!7i13312!8i6656?entry=ttu) also use “ALTO”, but the font is thinner.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/panama/11.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/iWGgnMeCtaC2KeYy5)

Red octagonal signs with an oddly **realistic eye** are unique to Panama.

### Step 2 - Regional clues

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/panama/12.png)](https://www.plonkit.net/images/panama/12.png)

While Panama is quite well covered, there is barely any coverage on the north coast, or east of Panama City.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/panama/13.png)](https://www.plonkit.net/images/panama/13.png)

The first digit of phone numbers can be used to regionguess Panama.

The numbers with a smaller font indicate potentially useful [regional two-digit codes](https://goo.gl/maps/fgoa9tJUxY5QL2C56).

NOTE: Only phone numbers with 7 digits are helpful, and any numbers starting with 5, 6, or 8 are useless as well.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/panama/14.png)](https://www.plonkit.net/images/panama/14.png)

The first digit of taxi and bus licence plates can be used to regionguess Panama.

NOTE: Cars travel, so they are not always reliable, especially outside of cities.

#### Landscape

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/panama/15.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/xeTP9RXkqCJHPtQVA)

**Green tropical landscapes** with palm trees and very tall grassare common around Colón and Panama City.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/panama/16.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/2UwdWeq3ne9XYdST8)

**Volcanic rocks** that are either scattered on the ground, or built into **rock walls**, are common north of David.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/panama/17.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/xZ3EPeQuJDzn4paG9)

The area around David is **very flat**, with lots of fairly **open fields**. Usually, [mountains](https://goo.gl/maps/MT8CapwfA7XwFeDw9) will be visible to the north and northeast.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/panama/18.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/s4RD9WRpQqoTJHXPA)

The region around Aguadulce is relatively **flat** and features a lot of agricultural fields along with very **brown or orange** soil.

#### Infrastructure

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/panama/19.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/yi7z3ui4vv4qy86N8)

The eastern provinces often have tall vertical plates that can be yellow or white. They are commonly attached to poles by black zip ties.

NOTE: Be careful not to mix them up with [these white vertical plates](https://goo.gl/maps/9UssGMTvSrtsLBN98), often attached by metallic zip ties. They are slightly wider than the eastern plates and always have 6 digits.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/panama/20.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/ZddsBP4tzehW1faL6)

Many poles in the western provinces will have **horizontal** plates attached to them. However, if one of these is accompanied by an [eastern vertical plate](https://maps.app.goo.gl/Fd541FefRusFDcE37), the eastern plate takes precedence.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/panama/21.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/c16Ro4gVBaG6bR1U9)

Thin yellow plates, vertical or horizontal, with a visible black border are most common in the Colón Province.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/panama/22.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/Rigy2UWGfdKTj2Px8)

Wind turbines can be seen [south](https://goo.gl/maps/NFm7beZBXA6frwMb7) and [north](https://goo.gl/maps/tbfJ4v7xXsSbRa4B8) of Penonome.

#### Distinct Roads

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/panama/23.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/z1GPPhxrTVsMSfVv7)

The main covered road in the Ngäbe-Buglé province is very **high-altitude** and almost above the clouds. The surrounding mountains tend to be quite **dry**, and the altitude makes for many stunning views.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/panama/24.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/VR6hqMA8fgzZ9guB8)

The road going to Changuinola features a lush and **green hilly landscape**. The weather is **cloudy** or overcaston the entire road.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/panama/25.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/FdYKUneX7Wb42MWYA)

Highway 9 going from Panama City north to Colón is a **divided concrete highway**. The road is visibly **grainy** and has a lot of **tar lines**. The landscape is lush and hilly.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/panama/26.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/AEgxDqRbctstA3mq5)

Most of Road 407 features a **freshly paved road**, with visibly **orange soil** on the sides of the road. The southern portion is [hilly](https://goo.gl/maps/jAhhHN6t4w56XvCM6), right before the road quality [deteriorates](https://goo.gl/maps/F59mLWj9TrUS9LXM8).

#### Car Meta

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/panama/27.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/F2Ln5kBkD5Xjw9Rb8)

A small **cluster of dirt** on the car can be found on the eastern road.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/panama/28.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/YbH3pFTAjwtnqXdz8)

In a lot of central Panama, the Google car will have either one or two **black dots visible** on the front.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/panama/29.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/mML2PEtost4THc3y6)

This **small red spot** can mostly be found in Panamá Oeste and on the road 2 going from southern Los Santos province to the Verguas province.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/panama/30.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/4adjzkFs7pM2Xo3L6)

The antenna can be seen **folded down**, most commonly in Colón and Panamá provinces, and occasionally in the city of David.

### Step 3 - Spotlight

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/panama/31.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/dg3jRgrwhNKeHsh49)

Panama City can be recognised by the large number of **white high-rise buildings**.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/panama/32.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/Ug54DTcAf9aw8nCCA)

Colón features a lot of **warehouses** and **rundown multi-storey buildings**.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/panama/33.png)](https://www.google.com/maps/@9.4121126,-82.5565031,3a,83.8y,258.62h,91.05t/data=!3m7!1e1!3m5!1sp-bU73xBVkMUSHGbbBbELg!2e0!6shttps:%2F%2Fstreetviewpixels-pa.googleapis.com%2Fv1%2Fthumbnail%3Fpanoid%3Dp-bU73xBVkMUSHGbbBbELg%26cb_client%3Dmaps_sv.tactile.gps%26w%3D203%26h%3D100%26yaw%3D93.29357%26pitch%3D0%26thumbfov%3D100!7i16384!8i8192?entry=ttu)

The region around Changuinola is filled with **banana plantations.**

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/panama/34.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/MVJ3m2z8nWphRksQ6)

**Sunset** coverage can be found in southern Panama on a small road south of Tonosi.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/panama/35.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/Rz1gbtuWRGqezNEt5)

A mountainouslandscape with **pine trees**, **volcanic rocks**, **dry grass**, and gravel roads can be found northeast of Volcán.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/panama/36.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/GQ5q4RLXSAAFzbuy8)

**Yellow pole socks** can be found near the Panama Canal, around the town of Gatún.
