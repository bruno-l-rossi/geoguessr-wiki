### Step 1 - Identifying Croatia

![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/croatia/transparent-hr_summary_2026.png)

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/croatia/Croatia_License_Plate.png)](https://www.plonkit.net/images/croatia/Croatia_License_Plate.png)

Croatia has long, **white licence plates**. Some plates have a blue strip on the left, some are fully white. Most cars seen in Generation 3 will not feature a blue strip on their licence plates. In addition, there is a mix of cars with or without blue strips in Generation 4, with blue strips more common on newer cars.

NOTE: Blue strips are more common along the coast due to travelling tourists from other EU countries such as Italy.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/croatia/Croatia_bollard.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/otJfSAyLgUKdnS8AA)

Croatian **bollards** are white with a black rectangle and red or white reflector. From the top, it is shaped like a triangle.

NOTE: Hungarian and Lithuanian bollards look similar**,** but have more **rounded** edges compared to Croatia’s sharper edges. Lithuanian bollards will also have an orange reflector. North Macedonia sometimes uses similar bollards, however the country is only covered in Generation 3.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/croatia/hr_language.png)](https://www.plonkit.net/images/croatia/hr_language.png)

Croatian is a **Slavic** language. It is always written in the Latin alphabet. ‘IJE’ is common in Croatian whereas Serbian uses only ‘E’ instead.

NOTE: The Slovene language also features a lot of ‘J’s. Serbian and Montenegrin are also very similar, but Serbia and Montenegro use both Latin and Cyrillic script.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/croatia/Chevron.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/x5SeUr9RSXiNm2tz5)

Croatia features chevrons with a **red** arrowon a **yellow** or **white** background.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/croatia/Croatia_guardrails.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/wbAEbA8oBYsZfjmJ7)

Croatia has **square (B-type) guardrails**.

NOTE: Croatia is one of the few European countries that use B-type guardrails. For a good overview of European guardrails, see [this infographic.](https://www.plonkit.net/images/croatia/europeguardrail.png)

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/croatia/Croatia-side-mirror.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/FfoksiH3oq1QLjqG8)

Generation 3 coverage often has a non-standard car blur where the **side mirrors** of the car are partially visible. This is not unique to Croatia, but by far most common there.

NOTE: Sometimes, you can see a small circular [**yellow** sticker](https://goo.gl/maps/8T97sBibVM3pgwEZ9) on the car mirror. This sticker is unique to Croatia.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/croatia/Fall_colours.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/awmnYbXpxo9LrtUi9)

Another quirk of Generation 3 coverage is that the majority of it was made in **autumn**. This means that the vegetation in this coverage will often have distinct yellowish or orange fall colours.

Take note that this seasonal meta is not found in Generation 4 coverage.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/croatia/Screenshot%20(625).png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/qjgLDkr2m6GwcjDLA)

A black generation 4 car that will never have an antenna can be found in Croatia.

NOTE: A black car can be found in many European countries, though it will often have an antenna visible.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/croatia/Screenshot%20(628).png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/24VTV3ZrWcy9XTqQ8)

A white generation 4 car that will never have an antenna can be found in Croatia.

NOTE: A white car can be found in many European countries, though it will often have an antenna visible.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/croatia/Croatia_HR_sticker2.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/41QU4bwKbJcWqh8p6)

Stickers with the letters **HR** can be found on Croatian cars. HR stands for Hrvatska, the Croatian name for Croatia.

NOTE: These were mainly used before EU plates were introduced, meaning they will be less common in Generation 4.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/croatia/Croatia_direction_signs.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/UwRgqSfgwHqSzDSE6)

Croatia has direction signs with a **yellow background** and a **black** outline. They often have a pencil-shaped tip.

NOTE: The other former Yugoslav countries of Slovenia, Serbia, Montenegro and North Macedonia have similar signs. A comparison can be seen [here](https://www.plonkit.net/images/croatia/croatia_directionsigncomp.png).

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/croatia/Croatia_poles.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/14H3ancaShKQKrUC6)

Croatian utility poles are cylindrical and made of concrete or wood, often with **alternating hooks** on the [wooden poles](https://goo.gl/maps/tudAitHkDfNSgbQu5) (somewhat similar to Latvian poles).

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/croatia/Croatia_red_brick.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/mrt8YkuJGjY9ZhBbA)

Buildings in Croatia sometimes feature exposed **red brick walls**. This architecture is more common in inland areas.

NOTE: This type of architecture can also be found in Slovenia and Serbia.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/croatia/image.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/SYjs6bE5sHjeZzK46)

Croatia features **blue** fire hydrants with two diagonal protrusions on either side. Newer fire hydrants seen in Generation 4 have a [smaller top](https://www.google.com/maps/@45.3444374,19.0031362,3a,15y,342.71h,79.67t/data=!3m6!1e1!3m4!1sPvq5ffNHeVxmDooenAuoyA!2e0!7i16384!8i8192) but are still recognisable due to the blue paint.

NOTE: Similar fire hydrants can be found in bordering countries, most commonly in Hungary.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/croatia/Pedestrian_sign_2.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/cD9GoZaSWcTx7qRg9)

Croatia’s pedestrian sign features a **pedestrian with a belt** and a zebra path with 5 stripes.

NOTE: The pedestrian on Slovenia’s sign does not have a belt.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/croatia/beers.webp)](https://goo.gl/maps/JvJTUodoeX9WQgH77)

**Karlovačko** and **Ožujsko** are common Croatian beer companies, with advertisements seen around the country.

**Konzum** and **Plodine** are common supermarket chains, mostly found in big cities. Plodine is exclusive to Croatia, while Konzum also operates stores in Bosnia.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/croatia/Croatia_gen_2_trekker.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/cPhvkPuv9C6W4ZaP7)

Croatia does not have Generation 2 car coverage. However, in some historic downtown areas, a **Generation 2 trekker** camera was used.

NOTE: In some of this trekker coverage, the Google employee’s red cap is visible.

### Step 2 - Regional and county-specific clues

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/croatia/hr_areacodes_copy.png)](https://www.plonkit.net/images/croatia/hr_areacodes_copy.png)

Croatian landlines typically start with a zero followed by the **area code**.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/croatia/Hungary_border_and_Slavonia.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/76BVKpj4AfLqYsrt7)

The north-eastern region of Croatia is mostly **flat** and features a lot of agriculture. It is similar in terms of landscape to Hungary and north-western Serbia.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/croatia/Zagreb_hill_farm_cool.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/QjtwpW6EsVLEGSPh7)

The region north and west of Zagreb is **hilly** and **agricultural**.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/croatia/Adriatic_coast_transparent_v2.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/N8JgCgFa6BP6tks17)

The Adriatic coast features **rocky** mountains similar to those seen in Montenegro or Albania. Shrubs are more common instead of trees along the coast, especially in the south. This coastal region has a lot of islands and peninsulas.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/croatia/Croatia_adriatic_brick_v2.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/3DJamyp9WYosjhvA9)

Houses and walls made of **beige brick** are common on the Adriatic coast. They often feature **green** shutters.

NOTE: Similar architecture can also be found in some regions in Italy. Additionally, Croatian shutters can also be white, beige etc.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/croatia/Istria.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/WLxSFw6H5PRLs2Kq9)

The north-western region of Istria, south of the Italian city of Trieste, sometimes has distinct **red** soil.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/croatia/Indicator-template-Croatia12.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/uw2x4PdMaqHEoEDn9)

Bilingual street name signs with Croatian and **Italian** are typically found in Istria, especially in the western cities.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/croatia/hr_ec.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/ZSqmMoGpVuuhAMTx9)

Town names ending with “ **\-ec** ” are common to see in the northern parts of central Croatia.

### Step 3 - Spotlight

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/croatia/Zagreb.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/tYzVscsQ7T1fEnHH9)

Croatia’s capital Zagreb has a distinct **big city feel**. Apartmentblocks can be seen throughout the city. The northern part of the city is hilly. Furthermore, Zagreb has [blue electric trams](https://goo.gl/maps/HKezsyENV7j4TJ1J7).

NOTE: In addition to regular car coverage, Zagreb also has various urban [sidewalk trekkers](https://goo.gl/maps/55CGuPvKbjzGLv4XA), often made with a Generation 2 camera.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/croatia/hr_trams.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/b8KiaANX7YRz3fGB8)

Zagreb and Osijek are the only two cities in Croatia with **trams**.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/croatia/Flat_Area_Northeast_Of_Zadar.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/k2tsuhYRdJJcD4Vx7)

The region northeast of Zadar is recognizable as it is flat with a **mountain** ridge visible in the distance north.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/croatia/Ilok_Vineyards.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/P7jfMhVpiLzvnZVj9)

There are a lot of vineyards around the town of **Ilok**, near the Serbian **border**,west of Novi Sad. You can find vineyards in other parts of Croatia as well, but they are most common in this area. If you see lots of vineyards in a northeastern Croatian landscape, you are more often than not near Ilok.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/croatia/long_bois.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/HNyMVsjf2FA54gPt5)

Traffic lights in Rijeka are held up by **long blue poles** that extend out over the road. These are exclusive to the city.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/croatia/lecters_house.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/Ar3MPd6Kf9UJcw7h8)

The town of Petrinja was severely damaged by an earthquake in 2020. As a result, many buildings can be seen **ruined** and undergoing repair.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/croatia/Dubrovnik-city-walls.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/D5oruJYfqox1UFzb9)

The city of Dubrovnik in the far south of coastal Croatia is famous for its beautifully preserved **medieval city walls**, which can be seen from many points in and around the Old Town.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/croatia/hr_dubrovniktrees.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/zWxsrtyeZnJ4Vtw58)

These tall **cypress trees** are commonly found near the southern tip of Croatia.
