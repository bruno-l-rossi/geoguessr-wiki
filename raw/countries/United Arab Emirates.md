### Step 1 - Identifying the UAE

![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/united-arab-emirates/0_Normal_Summary_Graphic_1.png)

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/united-arab-emirates/ae_desert.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/VNePq383S8THMzvK9)

The UAE typically features a flat **desert landscape**.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/united-arab-emirates/ae_roadlines.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/GV3fzE8unLEPjDDm6)

Outer roadlines are **yellow**, middle lines either **white** or **yellow**.

NOTE: Other Middle Eastern countries, like Jordan, Israel and Oman, also use these lines.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/united-arab-emirates/gen3car.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/48v58xgVdQb64m2e6)

In almost all Generation 3 coverage, a **white car** with visible black roof racks was used.

NOTE: The UAE is the only Middle Eastern country where a white Google car can be found.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/united-arab-emirates/2022-12-14_1__281_29.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/iKpxf8krJdtA16MBA)

In Generation 4 coverage, a white **pickup truck** with a trekker camera was used for much of the coverage. In a lot of the coverage, the truck is completely [blurred out](https://maps.app.goo.gl/gz9p2N59bhqFxtvT7).

NOTE: A similar truck can be found in [Qatar](https://maps.app.goo.gl/9vAjK8jA8Upvm1pq8) and [Oman](https://maps.app.goo.gl/CAfekUi6QZ6TF6bV7). In the UAE the antenna at the front of the cabin is on the middle of the car, while in Qatar it is on the left.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/united-arab-emirates/UAE_Gen_4_black.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/FwkQNmdyYHL74xj8A)

You can also find regular Generation 4 coverage taken with a **black** car. The brake light is commonly visible.

NOTE: A similar Generation 4 black car can be found in [Israel](https://maps.app.goo.gl/8AR2EPX61mXVjE1M6).

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/united-arab-emirates/arabic_language.png)](https://www.plonkit.net/images/united-arab-emirates/arabic_language.png)

The official language in the UAE is Arabic. Its script can be identified by its **lines and dots**.

NOTE: Due to high tourism, signs in the UAE often have English writing alongside Arabic. While this is also common in Qatar and Oman, it is not common in Jordan.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/united-arab-emirates/architecture.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/NvUYm7ieMn9DArty5)

Houses in residential areas are typically made of **light-coloured stone**. Reddish stones are often used for sidewalks.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/united-arab-emirates/ae_signpost.png)](https://www.google.com/maps/@25.0611912,55.707767,3a,15y,60.49h,82.37t/data=!3m6!1e1!3m4!1swL1j3pr02vFm9tlwF_OqpA!2e0!7i16384!8i8192?entry=ttu)

Many signposts in the UAE have **black-and-white stripes**.

NOTE: Within the Middle East, striped signposts are also found in Oman.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/united-arab-emirates/ae_kerbs.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/xc3nWyoNAAB282bt6)

Similarly, almost all curbs are **striped**. They can be either black-and-white, or black-and-yellow.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/united-arab-emirates/roundabout.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/6cSPp9Gkvxqonzna8)

**Divided roads** and **roundabouts** are both very commonly seen in the UAE.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/united-arab-emirates/ae_poles.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/SutBev8evrttFCzJA)

Electricity poles in the UAE are mostly made of **dark brown wood**. The pole tops consist of **three insulators**, symmetrically attached to a **horizontal metal bar**.

NOTE: Similar poletops can be found in Oman. Jordanian poletops may look similar, but they are usually made of grey concrete, and the insulators are more spaced out.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/united-arab-emirates/ae_unpavedresidentialroad.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/jodYR8yVoZGDboudA)

Although the UAE is a wealthy country, it is not uncommon for residential roads to be **unpaved**.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/united-arab-emirates/rifts.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/wdeVJfVcqZrg6iaT7)

**Rifts** can occasionally be found in the UAE.

NOTE: They are most commonly found in Albania, Montenegro and Senegal.

### Step 2 - Regional and emirate-specific clues

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/united-arab-emirates/ae_coverage.png)](https://www.plonkit.net/images/united-arab-emirates/ae_coverage.png)

Coverage in the UAE is mostly limited to the **Northeastern part** of the country.

NOTE: The only car coverage outside of this region exists on Sir Baniyas Island. All other remote coverage was taken by a trekker.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/united-arab-emirates/ae_dubaiskyline.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/o1QUCUumRMJSeryQ7)

The skyline of Dubai is dominated by numerous **skyscrapers**, including the iconic Burj Khalifa.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/united-arab-emirates/ae_eastmountains.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/qu584qCbnNes1GQ36)

**Mountains** are only found in the far east of the country.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/united-arab-emirates/Landscape_-_Ras_al_Khaimah_Mountains.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/1B3KYMU5tXCFow9f6)

You can easily recognise the Northern part of the emirate of Ras al Khaimah thanks to the **light-coloured mountains** visible to the east.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/united-arab-emirates/ae_gulfofoman.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/V49TXbk3K4RWqp736)

If you see **mountains to the west**, and flatness to the east, you are likely near the Gulf of Oman, in the extreme East of the country.

### Step 3 - Spotlight

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/united-arab-emirates/UAE-sir-baniyas.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/jNvE6bwu1SykMSKh9)

This rare white Google car with visible side mirrors and sparse vegetation is found on **Sir Baniyas** Island.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/united-arab-emirates/UAE-dubai-gen-4-trekker.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/548ZpVchkR3jcGju7)

In some parts of Dubai, including the [old town](https://goo.gl/maps/CXsP5xq4fAtX7Ds47), a Generation 4 **sidewalk trekker** was used. Sometimes, the employee’s black hair is visible.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/united-arab-emirates/ae_dubaisign.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/pqM2HBPmEjp7WEdp7)

These **blue** street name signs, with a narrow **metal border**, are specific to Dubai.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/united-arab-emirates/UAE-al-ain-trekkers.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/xCZKgEXBKALugf2J6)

The city of **Al Ain** has several trekkers, far away from the area with car coverage: two forts ([Qasr Al Muwaiji](https://goo.gl/maps/nbGPsLFqTb1terfh9) and [Al Jahili](https://goo.gl/maps/xMzzvnsvnPqw5ywR9)) and a [zoo](https://goo.gl/maps/XAVgfZVCEW1VPkUE9).

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/united-arab-emirates/UAE-racetrack.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/aS9bsLjJ6cYhLGdH9)

This **racetrack** coverage is found slightly east of Abu Dhabi.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/united-arab-emirates/UAE-southern-desert-trekkers.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/mG5v9HXoYZzx9MSN8)

Remote trekkers, with **sand dunes** all around, can be found in the far south of the country, between the towns of Taraq and Alyhyali.
