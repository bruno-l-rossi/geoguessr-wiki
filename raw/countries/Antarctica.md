### Step 1 - Identifying Antarctica

![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/antarctica/0_aq_summary.png)

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/antarctica/aq_snow.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/WdvUjKikrvCAbKnTA)

Most of Antarctica is covered in **snow and ice**.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/antarctica/aq_darksoil.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/rX871TMMebN15wqg9)

The soil will always be **dark**.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/antarctica/aq_pingwings.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/aAvFMEQ8jfkek3M88)

**Penguins** can be found on parts of the coverage.

NOTE: This is mostly useful to exclude coverage in the Arctics.

### Step 2 - Regional clues

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/antarctica/aq_halfmoonisland.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/FSAzovZcXiTLsyHL6)

Half Moon Island was covered by a **tripod**. It can also be recognized by the numerous other people in red jackets.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/antarctica/aq_deceptionisland.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/Gj7cPhS9x5U15ugp6)

The **Deception Island Bay** can be recognized by the blue and white ship in the bay, and the abandoned equipment on the beach.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/antarctica/aq_deceptionislandconga.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/swvB96kQYetBxFwy8)

The trekker can be seen **walking in a line** on Deception Island.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/antarctica/aq_deceptionislanddarkbeach.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/sqvn11NCvto2voh6A)

A beach with **black sand** and many penguins can be seen on Deception Island.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/antarctica/aq_cuvervilleisland.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/MM4jjEUWScfLfeaRA)

Cuverville Island can be recognised by the overcast weather combined with the coastline to the **northwest**. You’ll also see a decent number of penguins, particularly [toward the east](https://goo.gl/maps/EVEBMqPQCrBaeptV6).

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/antarctica/aq_dancoisland.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/gBuhotZS2bQfrf3K7)

Danco Island can be recognised by the **beautiful sunset coverage** over a snowy fjord.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/antarctica/aq_petermannisland.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/WUnCLoJWf27e4EUv8)

Petermann Island can be recognized by the layer of snow with patches of **exposed rocks**, and the overexposed camera.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/antarctica/aq_rossisland.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/q1ELtwJAtkANiymK8)

The coverage at the western tip of Ross Island can typically be recognised by the extremely dark, and mostly barren, **rocky soil**.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/antarctica/aq_rossislandcabin.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/uPXAtuGAxSUEUy9k6)

A tripod featuring the exterior and [interior](https://goo.gl/maps/s9oJFd4Q8Vuretn89) of a small wooden cabin with a **grey metal roof** can be found south of the eastern tip of Ross Island.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/antarctica/aq_mcmurdo.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/v1NYrq3KVTwiQoSH6)

McMurdo Station is the largest research station on Antarctica, and can be recognized by the **dark brown soil**, **many buildings**, American infrastructure and sunset coverage.

NOTE: A lot of the coverage around McMurdo Station has the sun setting in the south, which shouldn’t lead you to guess in the northern hemisphere.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/antarctica/aq_mcmurdotripods.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/HcARGup685XbAc4FA)

You can also find tripod coverage inside [several](https://goo.gl/maps/Pa8CQWgr7CmcTBvt7) [buildings](https://goo.gl/maps/JLbjTgt5ibVGp7SF8) at McMurdo Station.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/antarctica/aq_mcmurdohut.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/NizvjKX7WiCXrc2VA)

On the eastern side of McMurdo Station you can find a small wooden cabin, having a [tripod going inside](https://goo.gl/maps/LfHyR3gHdYmXM7TBA).

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/antarctica/aq_icetrekker.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/hTguZarvbp4eNazB7)

Southeast of McMurdo station, the trekker can be seen walking on a layer of ice around **various machinery**.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/antarctica/aq_mainlandvalleys.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/p1n1tgcGbNEa3J5fA)

The trekker can be seen walking through a fantastical landscape consisting of many **desolate valleys** on mainland Antarctica, west of Ross Island.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/antarctica/aq_blackisland.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/3w5rtf8c1hvmRAZo9)

A **vast, snowy landscape** with spread out hills can be seen south of McMurdo station.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/antarctica/aq_southpole.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/bPnDUuJDiiSAtmoY6)

The tripod at the **south pole** can be recognized by numerous countries’ flags around the tripod, and a sign with ‘Geographic South Pole’ written on it.

This location is often on community-made world maps for a better scoring range.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/antarctica/aq_southpoleobservatory.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/tubFheZLxov1FxXz9)

The South Pole Observatory has tripod coverage going around and inside of it.
