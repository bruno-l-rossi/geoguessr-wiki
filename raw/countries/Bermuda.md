### Step 1 - Identifying Bermuda

![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/bermuda/0_bm_summary.png)

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/bermuda/bm_location.png)](https://www.plonkit.net/images/bermuda/bm_location.png)

Bermuda is a British overseas territory, located north of the Caribbean sea. As such, it is counted as **the United Kingdom** for country streaks purposes.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/bermuda/bm_lefthanddrive.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/6v3gTTJcpigMYPLh8)

Bermuda drives on the **left side** of the road.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/bermuda/bm_googlecar.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/i11ygQrbjMXY2DhM7)

Bermuda features a **black** Street View car with the entire front sides of the vehicle typically visible.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/bermuda/bm_generallook.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/BuMojmzaEiaX8WNR9)

The most typical look of Bermuda is somewhat dense vegetation, consisting of fairly small trees and a lot of bushes, combined with single family houses strewn across the landscape.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/bermuda/bm_licenceplate.png)](https://www.plonkit.net/images/bermuda/bm_licenceplate.png)

Bermudan licence plates are all-white, fairly long, and with black digits, somewhat contained to the middle of the plate.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/bermuda/bm_yellowroadline.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/7JmEyBcA9tZu3bbp9)

Most roads in Bermuda contain a **single solid yellow middle line** with no outer road lines.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/bermuda/bm_zigzaglines.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/BJWKZjNXHyD98rPi8)

You will occasionally see these white **zig zag road lines** on Bermuda.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/bermuda/bm_architecture.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/bvdVkzPJxUe6hb6b7)

Most buildings on Bermuda are single family stone houses painted in **pastel colours**. They will almost always have **completely white roofs**.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/bermuda/bm_palms.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/zZoF8ML6ryHYvarw7)

**Palms** are very commonly found on Bermuda.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/bermuda/bm_bluewater.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/VmzYgr8D1MSUKuG9A)

On Bermuda you will never be far away from the ocean, and the water will very often look **distinctly light blue**.

### Step 2 - Regional clues

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/bermuda/bm_downtownhamilton.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/qF86PbQfiAmsMTsi6)

Hamilton is by far the largest town on Bermuda, and as such, the downtown area is distinct due to its many large, densely built **multi-floor buildings**.

### Step 3 - Spotlight

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/bermuda/bm_northwestpeninsula.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/dXekNBoUDpv6gQYm7)

The northwestern tip of Bermuda can be recognised by the many large buildings made of **unpainted grey brick**.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/bermuda/bermuda_boat.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/LLyY7aLLpAgTsQeMA)

A **boat trekker** can be seen on the eastern coast of Bermuda. It can be seen going around [Saint George’s Harbour](https://goo.gl/maps/U6Lze1J5ehU1tJqr9) and past [Fort St. Catherine](https://goo.gl/maps/9UciXRzs9WeyiRpv6) on the northeast tip of the island.
