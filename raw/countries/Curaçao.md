### Step 1 - Identifying Curaçao

![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/curacao/0_summary_curacao.png)

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/curacao/1920px-Curacao_in_its_region.svg.png)](https://www.plonkit.net/images/curacao/1920px-Curacao_in_its_region.svg.png)

Curaçao is a constituent country in the Kingdom of the Netherlands located north of western Venezuela in the Caribbean Sea. As such, for country streaks purposes, it is counted as the Netherlands.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/curacao/image.png)](https://www.plonkit.net/images/curacao/image.png)

Curaçao uses square licence plates that appear completely white through the blur.

NOTE: While front plates are mandatory, a [minority](https://goo.gl/maps/rXKj6bBALkcMM5Cg9) will still not have front plates.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/curacao/image__284_29.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/o4zB8eW4K96Cd9bV9)

This **black car** with a **roof rack** is unique to Curaçao.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/curacao/cw_cacti.webp)](https://goo.gl/maps/2WEdmyNf3ohqPYBJ9)

Due to Curaçao’s semi-arid climate, **cacti** can commonly be seen.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/curacao/image__281_29.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/vfu6drnkyNZH1ywMA)

Much of the architecture on Curaçao consists of **simple, one storey bungalows** that are **pastel coloured**. Metal roofs are common on smaller shacks, while tiled roofs can be seen on more expensive houses. Additionally, multi storey villas can sometimes be seen.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/curacao/cw_signbackborder.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/1PqPxt6KXaVMtKyN7)

The coloured border or road signs can typically be seen continuing onto the back of the signs. These will generally be red or blue.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/curacao/cw_yellowsignpost.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/kpgkjQhcV36khysS6)

Blue street signs attached to the top of **yellow signposts** are common in Curaçao.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/curacao/image__282_29.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/52UqrVVx2iqovBds7)

**White and black** striped traffic light posts can be seen in urban areas.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/curacao/image__283_29.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/CFpdApuPubbjrHWe7)

Wooden poles with **three horizontal**, **alternating** insulators are common.

NOTE: These poletops can also be found in Brazil, South Africa, and Eswatini.

### Step 2 - Regional clues

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/curacao/rural.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/972dfR9bMJXSARF2A)

The western half of Curaçao is characterised by a semi-arid **rural** **landscape** with green, but dry hills. Houses are spread apart, with the roads in the west quite narrow in width.

### Step 3 - Spotlight

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/curacao/wind.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/j9NGzSbQVKaPbUo89)

A line of **wind turbines** can be seen at this wind farm toward the southeast of Curaçao. The coverage is also notably rainy.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/curacao/bridge.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/5ZA2PefSwkeFfvFi7)

The **Queen Juliana Bridge** is a wide four lane bridge in Willemstad. It can be recognized by the yellow and blue guardrails, and the view over the city.
