### Step 1 - Identifying Malta

![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/malta/0_Normal_Summary_Graphic.png)

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/malta/mt_drivingside.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/rfZ8SAafi1SBttnA9)

Malta drives on the **left**.

NOTE: Cyprus also drives on the left.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/malta/mt_licenceplate.png)](https://www.plonkit.net/images/malta/mt_licenceplate.png)

Like most of Europe, Maltese licence plates have a blue strip on the left. However, unlike most of Europe, **short plates** are very common.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/malta/mt_landscape.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/T3QxnVYSJTAy2HE36)

The general landscape of Malta consists of slightly hilly **dry agricultural fields**. Many roads are also only 1 or 2 lanes wide, due to the island’s size. Having a mediterranean climate, [cacti](https://maps.app.goo.gl/Kh2pMFPUTQhivKT76#extra%5Btags%5D=yes) and various [dry shrubs](https://maps.app.goo.gl/BqgdrBxdG2UFft3z7#extra%5Btags%5D=yes) can be seen.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/malta/mt_rockwall.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/my8PqqA57SB2pbDc6#extra%5Btags%5D=yes)

**Limestone rock walls** are extremely common in Malta. This alone is usually enough to determine that you are in Malta.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/malta/mt_architecture.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/e19YM7JpiyDN11a89)

The vast majority of buildings in Malta consist of low storey buildings, with **white to beige** coloured walls. Buildings made of [limestone](https://maps.app.goo.gl/BMSfQdQ7rujtEErg8#extra%5Btags%5D=yes) are also fairly common.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/malta/mt_balconies.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/yUJShYjxa1mhUeWN6)

This style of traditional **covered balconies** is very common on Maltese buildings. They are often painted in bright colours.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/malta/mt_triq.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/MEuWmXYJ3izUoE2v5)

The Maltese word for street is “ **triq** ”, which can be seen on almost every street sign.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/malta/mt_busstop.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/i7LEyjPaARkUwj2x5)

**Bus stops** are a common sight in Malta. They may sometimes have useful information as to where you are.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/malta/mt_doubleyellowline.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/FARDjAGrHppMShPQ7)

Like the UK and Ireland, Malta uses these distinctive **double yellow no parking lines**.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/malta/mt_english.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/Dmr5UrfEJtx7krTx6#extra%5Btags%5D=eng)

**English** is the primary language of Malta.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/malta/mt_language.png)](https://www.plonkit.net/images/malta/mt_language.png)

Apart from English it is also common to see the **Maltese** language. Maltese uses the Latin script, but with unique letters, which include:

- Ċ ċ
- Ġ ġ
- Ħ ħ
- Ż ż

### Step 2 - Regional clues

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/malta/Gozo_Guardrails.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/xFtiRDcMLwNwoRvu6)

Regular metal guardrails are rare on Gozo Island. **Low stone barriers** are more common instead.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/malta/Gozo_Bus_Stop.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/2F963MPw76pBqYAt7)

Uniquely, all bus routes on Gozo Island are in the **300s**. These can be quite hard to see, meaning they are most useful in moving games.

### Step 3 - Spotlight

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/malta/Valletta_Blue_Signs.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/VRkErHz2kwSuYg1R8)

These **blue signs** are unique to Valletta.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/malta/Boat.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/ndhsLqocWaJuh8Si7)

This trekker on a ship that is painted **white and blue** can be seen between the main island and Gozo Island.
