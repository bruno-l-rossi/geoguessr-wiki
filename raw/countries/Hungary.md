### Step 1 - Identifying Hungary

![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/hungary/transparent-hu_summary_2026.png)

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/hungary/Hungary_License_Plate.png)](https://www.plonkit.net/images/hungary/Hungary_License_Plate.png)

Hungary uses **long white** standard European licence plates. Taxi licence plates are **yellow.**

In July of 2022, Hungary switched to this new character format, which contains the coat of arms.

NOTE: Newer electric vehicles have green plates.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/hungary/hu_bollard.png)](https://www.google.se/maps/@47.1765642,18.80994,3a,36.6y,183.67h,66.04t/data=!3m6!1e1!3m4!1scybzctaIto3MgG1bEOvhmw!2e0!7i16384!8i8192!5m1!1e4)

Hungarian bollards are black-and-white, wedge-shaped, and typically have a **red** reflector at the front, and a white reflector at the back. [Blue reflectors](https://www.google.com/maps/@47.826971,20.8472466,3a,15y,282.66h,79.6t/data=!3m6!1e1!3m4!1s9lVB-fYRUINpTrMAYWvYZg!2e0!7i16384!8i8192) are also somewhat common.

NOTE: [Croatian bollards](https://www.google.se/maps/@44.9117293,15.1630293,3a,17.5y,144.5h,72.84t/data=!3m6!1e1!3m4!1sQHfQsXnyjPhm0AakdfHr0A!2e0!7i16384!8i8192) are very similar. They do, however, typically have the reflector closer to the top, and appear somewhat [thinner](https://www.google.se/maps/@44.9122903,15.1621345,3a,48.7y,222.73h,52.12t/data=!3m6!1e1!3m4!1sbWEK8Fssx79YL61MpgzMqg!2e0!7i16384!8i8192) than in Hungary.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/hungary/hu_pole.png)](https://www.google.com/maps/@47.6425626,20.0330594,3a,75y,313.1h,93.94t/data=!3m6!1e1!3m4!1sKeSZXDk8-kk4EYn1mVYf3Q!2e0!7i16384!8i8192)

The most common type of utility pole in Hungary is a **concrete holey pole**, with relatively thin holes going all the way down to the ground. Wooden poles are also somewhat common.

NOTE: In eastern Europe, Poland and Romania also commonly use holey poles. [Polish holey poles](https://www.google.com/maps/@49.9926761,18.7114721,3a,55.1y,130.81h,98.23t/data=!3m6!1e1!3m4!1sHtPKStzKxeiHUY1s-iNu-w!2e0!7i16384!8i8192) typically do not have holes down by the ground, and tend to be relatively thin. [Romanian holey poles](https://goo.gl/maps/QyJwGvUuzdnJLVBv5) normally either have much wider, or much taller holes.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/hungary/hu_chevron.png)](https://www.google.se/maps/@47.7241722,20.3412838,3a,15y,95.76h,79.54t/data=!3m6!1e1!3m4!1slyGHewQbh_nmuqTxRy8DxQ!2e0!7i16384!8i8192!5m1!1e4)

Hungarian chevrons are **white on red**.

[Black-and-white](https://www.google.com/maps/@47.2517674,16.9392096,3a,37.6y,261.49h,65.38t/data=!3m7!1e1!3m5!1s_Vx5ThXqoem-sKrW96OMFw!2e0!5s20111101T000000!7i13312!8i6656) chevrons may also be seen, but are considerably less common.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/hungary/hu_language.png)](https://www.plonkit.net/images/hungary/hu_language.png)

The **Hungarian language** has a unique look, with a very large number of [acute accents](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Acute_accent), [umlauts](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Umlaut_\(diacritic\)) over o’s, and long words. The letters "Ő" and "Ű", with double acute accents, are unique to the language.

NOTE: Hungarian is also spoken in some parts of neighbouring countries: notably there are large Hungarian-speaking communities in central Romania and southern Slovakia.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/hungary/hu_streetnames.png)](https://www.google.com/maps/@47.4088865,19.2121875,3a,19y,40.65h,71.88t/data=!3m6!1e1!3m4!1sW4d5IPH5I8O5KxzsIDT_KA!2e0!7i16384!8i8192)

Hungarian street names typically end with the word “ **utca** ”. The word “út” is also used for bigger roads. These two words are unique to Hungarian, and translate to “street.”

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/hungary/hu_roadmarkers.png)](https://www.google.se/maps/@46.445648,16.8206862,3a,15y,283.14h,79.9t/data=!3m6!1e1!3m4!1sjp680M6m9bFh-AnZqortGA!2e0!7i16384!8i8192)

These **green kilometre markers** can be seen along most Hungarian roads.

On highways, the numbers correspond to the ones seen on the map in small yellow boxes near exits.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/hungary/hu_signpostheight.png)](https://www.google.se/maps/@46.2611667,20.1438266,3a,44.9y,67.63h,84.13t/data=!3m7!1e1!3m5!1ssrwyQdFFMuFDEpPJqgp8EA!2e0!6shttps:%2F%2Fstreetviewpixels-pa.googleapis.com%2Fv1%2Fthumbnail%3Fpanoid%3DsrwyQdFFMuFDEpPJqgp8EA%26cb_client%3Dmaps_sv.tactile.gps%26w%3D203%26h%3D100%26yaw%3D253.7574%26pitch%3D0%26thumbfov%3D100!7i16384!8i8192!5m1!1e4)

Hungarian sign posts commonly appear to be **abnormally tall**, often taller than the Street View camera.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/hungary/hu_signbolts.png)](https://www.google.com/maps/@47.1888879,19.9992242,3a,15y,136.29h,88.75t/data=!3m6!1e1!3m4!1sZ6BTnNfuYipblClKxGCCGw!2e0!7i16384!8i8192)

Hungarian signs are usually bolted to the sign post using **four bolts**, going through the sign, in the shape of a tall rectangle.

NOTE: This is not common in any other Eastern European countries.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/hungary/hu_pedestriansign.png)](https://www.google.se/maps/@46.2585822,20.1428534,3a,15y,219.09h,96.83t/data=!3m6!1e1!3m4!1sth50roHT_cWDrCrh27H6AQ!2e0!7i16384!8i8192!5m1!1e4)

Hungarian pedestrian crossing signs have **five stripes**. The person is wearing a **belt**. There is also a second, less common, type with four stripes and the person holding a bag.

The belt is located noticeably **low** on the person’s body, almost as if the person is sagging his pants. This is unique to Hungary.

NOTE: Among neighbouring countries, Croatia and Slovakia also have five stripes and a belt.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/hungary/hu_roadshield.png)](https://www.google.com/maps/@46.1783043,19.2914553,3a,15y,343.37h,82.02t/data=!3m6!1e1!3m4!1sIo5k5Zu1KZZqqXJO6R8mNg!2e0!7i16384!8i8192)

Apart from European highways, there are two types of numbered roads in Hungary; roads marked with **green signs**, and highways beginning with the letter ‘ **M** ’, marked with **blue signs**. These road numbers are contained within a small shield shape on the sign.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/hungary/hu_directionalsigns.png)](https://www.google.se/maps/@47.7301932,21.6796284,3a,15y,31.07h,92.49t/data=!3m6!1e1!3m4!1sg2pvC6WhHQ60_QF-xaftGA!2e0!7i16384!8i8192)

Most directional signs are green, having a white inner border and a green outer border. Directional signs on M-numbered highways are blue, having a white inner border and a blue outer border.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/hungary/hu_flagantenna.png)](https://www.google.com/maps/@47.3386871,17.6059364,3a,29.7y,222.86h,53.18t/data=!3m7!1e1!3m5!1sEffVk4ARFe5eNFUQSh1YAQ!2e0!5s20111101T000000!7i13312!8i6656)

It is somewhat common to see a small, grey flag on the antenna of Generation 3 Google cars in Hungary.

NOTE: This is also seen in the Czech Republic, Slovakia, Romania and Bulgaria.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/hungary/Screenshot%20(621).png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/MEY3GbNRCjCxHwzT6)

A white generation 4 Google car can also rarely be found in Hungary.

NOTE: It can also be found in other European countries.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/hungary/Screenshot%20(623).png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/iYsd9HXqmueobFRQ6)

A black generation 4 car that will never have an antenna can be found in Hungary.

NOTE: A black car can be found in many European countries, though it will often have an antenna visible.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/hungary/hu_winter.png)](https://www.google.com/maps/@46.6023394,17.9601003,3a,75y,286.81h,93.94t/data=!3m6!1e1!3m4!1sOG1aVQJltpKL7j1HZTfTZw!2e0!7i13312!8i6656)

The majority of Generation 3 coverage in Hungary was taken **during winter**, resulting in very grey, dead looking coverage.

NOTE: This is also common in the Czech Republic and Bulgaria.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/hungary/hu_houses.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/urinijfqvXNis87BA)

Hungarian houses are typically made of stone, painted in a light colour and almost always with **red tiled roofs**. Suburban houses are typically only one storeyed, while more central houses may be taller. Houses in town and city centres are often fairly old but well-kept.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/hungary/hu_bicyclelanelines.png)](https://www.google.com/maps/@47.8601712,22.1052357,3a,28.9y,112.91h,82.95t/data=!3m6!1e1!3m4!1sqUuV4s6GakDRUHsiAmcOIg!2e0!7i16384!8i8192)

While Hungarian road lines are all-white, lines on **bicycle lanes** are typically **yellow**.

### Step 2 - Regional and county-specific clues

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/hungary/hu_areacodes.png)](https://www.plonkit.net/images/hungary/hu_areacodes.png)

Hungarian phone numbers consist of either 8 or 10 digits. If the number has 8 digits, the first and second digit is the **area code**. And if the number has 10 digits, the third and fourth digit is the area code. The only exception is Budapest, where only the first or third digit, being a 1, respectively is the area code.

NOTE: Area codes whose second digit is 0 or 1 are not region specific.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/hungary/hu_roadnumbers.png)](https://www.plonkit.net/images/hungary/hu_roadnumbers.png)

The first digit of Hungarian **numbered roads** are ordered in a circle, centred around Budapest. Remembering this system should make it quite easy to find any numbered road.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/hungary/hu_townnameprefixes.png)](https://www.plonkit.net/images/hungary/hu_townnameprefixes.png)

Many Hungarian town names use **local prefixes**, the most common of which are shown in this infographic. Note that some rare exceptions exist.

*You can click on the image to enlarge it.*

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/hungary/hu_streetsignmetalframe.png)](https://www.google.com/maps/@47.5495267,17.7489717,3a,15y,295.8h,70.97t/data=!3m6!1e1!3m4!1sx_ia3S3xHwuoPu87XAlhWw!2e0!7i16384!8i8192)

If you see a street sign with a **thick metal frame** around it, you are very likely to be in the **western** parts of the country.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/hungary/hu_bluegreenarrowstreetsignsonutilitypolesthistitleistoodamnlong.png)](https://www.google.com/maps/@47.8397175,22.6813335,3a,22.8y,292.62h,93.92t,0.24r/data=!3m6!1e1!3m4!1s4zPIS9TWj1-NOJW9S76xfg!2e0!7i16384!8i8192)

If you see a **blue** or **green** street sign shaped like an **arrow**, attached to a utility pole or street light, you are likely to be in the county of Szabolcs-Szatmár-Bereg.

NOTE: Blue arrow-shaped street signs can also be found in [Dunakeszi](https://www.google.com/maps/@47.6290186,19.1549932,3a,29.7y,151.87h,90.55t/data=!3m6!1e1!3m4!1se_1iRMK0jnxi0R13E9x-8A!2e0!7i16384!8i8192), north of Budapest. However, those are attached to blue sign posts.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/hungary/hu_mountains.png)](https://www.google.com/maps/@48.0646963,20.6414962,3a,60.1y,147.76h,87.67t/data=!3m6!1e1!3m4!1s2Ebx8lUttroaf1Ua7UDqcA!2e0!7i16384!8i8192)

While the southeastern parts of Hungary are extremely flat, with almost no noticeable hills, the northern and western parts mainly consist of **forested hills and mountains**. The tallest mountains are found in the north, while the southwestern mountains are generally slightly lower.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/hungary/hu_balaton.png)](https://www.google.se/maps/@46.9060321,17.8944136,3a,90y,44.08h,85.79t/data=!3m7!1e1!3m5!1s7FzdpSfwbCur45pnmzKSVg!2e0!6shttps:%2F%2Fstreetviewpixels-pa.googleapis.com%2Fv1%2Fthumbnail%3Fpanoid%3D7FzdpSfwbCur45pnmzKSVg%26cb_client%3Dmaps_sv.tactile.gps%26w%3D203%26h%3D100%26yaw%3D3.5394573%26pitch%3D0%26thumbfov%3D100!7i16384!8i8192)

Lake Balaton is one of the largest lakes on mainland Europe, and the **only large lake** visible on street view in Hungary. The area surrounding the lake is moderately hilly, fairly built up and a big tourist destination, with a much higher density of small hotels than almost anywhere else in the country.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/hungary/hu_puszta.png)](https://www.google.com/maps/@47.5710114,21.2631741,3a,90y,211.65h,86.51t/data=!3m6!1e1!3m4!1su8rLhnNYdP8dr0H6RyXHxg!2e0!7i16384!8i8192)

Unlike most of the Hungarian plains, which is almost entirely cultivated land, the Puszta, centred around Hortobágy National Park, mainly consists of barren and wet, natural grasslands.
