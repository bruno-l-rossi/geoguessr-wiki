### Step 1 - Identifying Slovenia

![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/slovenia/0_summary_si.png)

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/slovenia/si_landscape.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/fyJgcu129vA9CXAf7)

The Slovenian landscape is generally very **hilly and forested**, with occasional agriculture.

*For exceptions to this, see the region guessing section.*

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/slovenia/si_forest.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/uD64Dz8ryEx8CyNt9)

A very significant portion of the coverage in Slovenia was taken on small roads deep **inside forest**.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/slovenia/si_architecture.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/NUUhTHLhNPzwZBmB6)

Slovenian architecture takes much inspiration from alpine architecture, with light coloured walls, **dark wooden details** and semi-steep roofs. The most common roof shape is [half-](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hip_roof#Half-hip_roof) [h](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hip_roof#Half-hip_roof) [ipped roofs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hip_roof#Half-hip_roof).

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/slovenia/si_language.png)](https://www.plonkit.net/images/slovenia/si_language.png)

Slovene is a **Slavic language** featuring the special letters Č, Š, and Ž. It is closely related to Croatian, Serbian and Montenegrin.

NOTE: On top of having the same letters as Slovene, Croatian also has the letters Ć and Đ.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/slovenia/si_bollard.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/SgAsnVQAUXyzWDNX6)

Slovenian bollards are white with a black top. The front reflector will typically be **bright red**, while the back reflector will be **white**.

NOTE: [Austrian](https://www.google.com/maps/@47.935857,15.6009415,3a,20.6y,357.69h,65.73t/data=!3m6!1e1!3m4!1sak-sxymIs2V63Wt8uwtlKA!2e0!7i16384!8i8192?entry=ttu) bollards have the same design, but with a dark red front reflector and a black or grey back reflector. [Montenegrin](https://maps.app.goo.gl/VKcVBeDadPfdZEwQ9) bollards also have a very similar design, also with a red and a white reflector.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/slovenia/si_directionalsigns.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/p7YCZEAJRUnThY888)

Slovenian directional signs are **yellow** with a **black border**.

NOTE: Croatia, North Macedonia, Serbia and Montenegro all have similar directional signs. [Austrian](https://www.google.com/maps/@46.5568458,13.9585166,3a,17.9y,222.77h,88.58t/data=!3m6!1e1!3m4!1sgiePX811VzIqDTkzzrcHsw!2e0!7i13312!8i6656?entry=ttu) directional signs are typically white with a blue outline.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/slovenia/si_kmmarker.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/M6BqRECK2KujvQRJ7)

Slovenian kilometre markers have a very distinct design. At the top left the road number is shown in a **yellow rectangle**, and a second number is to the right of it. A horizontal black line divides the top and bottom, and the distance is shown below.

NOTE: [Austrian](https://goo.gl/maps/jtRvnb9JCZBuW8iLA) kilometre markers are blue with a white number.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/slovenia/si_chevron.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/8desQcHxVxzZ1UdA9)

Slovenian chevrons usually have **red arrows** on a **white background**.

You can also find a less common [version](https://maps.app.goo.gl/3rjSbRq9SMVina6a7) with a **black arrow** on a **white background**.

NOTE: [Austrian](https://goo.gl/maps/BM8aPY3HC9ox4UNHA) chevrons are either white on red or red on yellow.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/slovenia/si_pedestriansign.png)](https://www.google.com/maps/@46.2518621,15.1661213,3a,15y,335.85h,94.61t/data=!3m6!1e1!3m4!1soKL9BE_1rXvBVMiYe2Pt2g!2e0!7i16384!8i8192?entry=ttu)

Slovenian pedestrian signs have **five stripes** and a simply drawn person without a belt.

NOTE: [Austrian](https://goo.gl/maps/S3nXofrbfKot9eLD6) pedestrian signs have two dotted lines, while the person on [Croatian](https://maps.app.goo.gl/yy9Gf53S8AH7Areg6) signs has a belt.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/slovenia/slovenia_roadlines.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/sqsTABWWShzcEte28)

Slovenian roads commonly feature **long outer dashes**.

NOTE: Neighbouring countries do not frequently use dashed outside lines.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/slovenia/si_streetsigns.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/o7dHGuHDXeAPfvfL7)

Slovenian street signs are most commonly white with a **metal border** attached to signposts, or **red** and attached to house walls.

NOTE: [Czech](https://goo.gl/maps/gi5f8C9LNAUumAQFA) street signs are also typically red and attached to walls. However, Czech street names will not contain the word “ulica”, which most Slovenian street names have.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/slovenia/si_housenumber.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/Lc4H9tgSMno264Vq8)

Slovenian house numbers are typically **red** with white text.

NOTE: [Czech](https://www.google.com/maps/@50.7257121,15.1788819,3a,15y,1.15h,98.69t/data=!3m6!1e1!3m4!1s4rpA0yUvp4TJif4QGtcHvQ!2e0!7i16384!8i8192?entry=ttu) house numbers are also commonly red.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/slovenia/si_guardrail.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/Qwtgvq1Yc3rN9mJb9)

Slovenia uses a regular A-type guardrail, like most European countries. This guardrail type has **rounded edges**.

This is mostly useful for 50-50s with Croatia, which uses B-type guardrails. This type has square edges.

NOTE: For a good overview of European guardrails, see [this infographic](https://www.plonkit.net/images/slovenia/europeguardrail.png).

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/slovenia/si_kozolec.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/12yrqkie2jTgHV9d6)

This type of shed, called a [kozolec](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hayrack), made of thin **wooden beams** with noticeable gaps in between, is very typical for Slovenia.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/slovenia/Screenshot%20(631).png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/6DUrNfj5o24bvwnv5)

A black generation 4 car can be found in Slovenia.

NOTE: A black car can be found in many European countries.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/slovenia/Screenshot%20(633).png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/mvQrptvFuc4jxNFn9)

A white generation 4 Google car can also rarely be found in Slovenia.

NOTE: It can also be found in other European countries.

### Step 2 - Regional clues

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/slovenia/si_mountains.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/oEiCcrEkAqx9QpLx8)

The tallest mountains in the country can be found in the northwestern **alpine** region.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/slovenia/si_eastplains.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/WHRP4HawsYzgD7XD8)

**Flat**, or rolling, agricultural fields with **no mountains or tall hills** in view can be seen in the east of the country.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/slovenia/si_coastallandscape.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/d1fwmv4U5y4X9iP46)

Near the coast you get a landscape similar to the rest of the Adriatic coast; **white cliffs** with bushy vegetation, vineyards and Mediterranean architecture.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/slovenia/si_southeastvalley.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/eHoYay8NwHHjpYJ2A)

A **wide valley** going east to west, with mountains to the north and south can be found in the southeast.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/slovenia/si_italian.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/YN1fjRJo9mUaB1m19)

**Italian bilingual signs** can be found in the westernmost region.

### Step 3 - Spotlight

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/slovenia/si_ljubljanahousenumber.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/pTMPQMesA31QVMHC6)

Instead of the normal red house numbers, Ljubljana will typically feature **green house numbers**.

NOTE: [Street signs](https://goo.gl/maps/h5GSwk121qRJantj8) in Ljubljana are also occasionally green.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/slovenia/si_celjestreetsigns.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/NPDebpmTJR3wg7R3A)

Street signs in Celje feature the emblem of the city, a blue shield with **three yellow stars**.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/slovenia/si_trbovljestreetsigns.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/12cy8Tdnyf2Cx9qy6)

Similar to Celje, Trbovlje also features the city’s coat of arms, which is a **blue and green** shield with a black crest.
