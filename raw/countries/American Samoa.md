### Step 1 - Identifying American Samoa

![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/american-samoa/0_Summary_Graphic.png)

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/american-samoa/as_locator.png)](https://www.plonkit.net/images/american-samoa/as_locator.png)

**American Samoa** is a group of islands east of Fiji, located in the Pacific Ocean. Since it is a US overseas territory, for country streak purposes, it is counted as the US.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/american-samoa/image.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/9J2TRoEJL2CfdnZF9)

The general landscape consists of **green, tropical** mountains with many palm trees. Small roads are often made of [concrete](https://goo.gl/maps/GENv4jmjUzvkSbp38). Most houses are one storey, and have a simple box design. Plain **metal sheet** roofs are also very common.

### Step 2 - Island specific clues

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/american-samoa/main_island_car.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/JsSbHdpLifbJ1Jdt8)

A slightly **rusted black** truck bed can be seen on the main island of Tutuila.

This is the most common car that will be seen as it has the most coverage.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/american-samoa/other_island_car.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/Ec2D9s8PzZsWwfkXA)

The front of a **silver-grey** truck with side mirrors can be seen on Manu’a and Olosega, which are connected by a bridge.

*See below for more information.*

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/american-samoa/other_other_island_car.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/89AgZvTc9HJjYqCk8)

The front of a similar truck can be seen on the easternmost covered island of Tau. However, the truck will be **glossy black**, with side mirrors visible.

### Step 3 - Spotlight

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/american-samoa/bride.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/KyriaPF11ZHRuwM47)

The **single-lane** bridge, connecting the islands of Manu’a and Olosega, can be easily recognized by the thin wire guardrail, with the two islands on either side of the bridge.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/american-samoa/boeing_real_estate.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/mK54EUK79dgWx7qv6)

Ofu Airport is small with a **concrete** runway. The Google Car covering it has two **orange straps** on the roof. Many palm trees can also be seen on the other side of the runway.
