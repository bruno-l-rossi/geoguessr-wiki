### Step 1 - Identifying Guam

![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/guam/0_gu_summary.png)

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/guam/gu_location.png)](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/75/Guam_in_Oceania_%28-mini_map_-rivers%29.svg/2388px-Guam_in_Oceania_%28-mini_map_-rivers%29.svg.png)

Guam is a United States overseas territory. As such, it is counted as **the US** for country streaks purposes.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/guam/gu_car.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/VRiC7utYWhZZjnsm9)

The entire Google car is blurred, however an **antenna** at the front right and a tiny part of the **roof rack** can be seen.

NOTE: You will not see a roof rack on the Northern Mariana Islands Google car.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/guam/gu_pole.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/jPup4bbtnzrAwtpM7)

Poles on Guam are almost always **concrete** and commonly very thick.

NOTE: Northern Mariana Islands typically have wooden poles.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/guam/streetsign.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/FCLP4g95945UNCAs8)

Guam uses **green** street signs, unlike the Northern Mariana Islands, which use blue street signs.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/guam/gu_landscape.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/dquWaVahW6qV5rSY8)

Guam has a **tropical climate** with a lot of dense and somewhat low vegetation.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/guam/gu_reflectorspam.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/rV74Bk4bBnfL1emp9)

Both Guam and Northern Mariana Islands often have a very **large number** of **road reflectors**.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/guam/gu_asian.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/5nqPw4zR28Bqm25w5)

**East Asian languages** such as Japanese, Chinese, Korean, and even Russian are common to see in Guam.

NOTE: This is also the case in Northern Mariana Islands.

### Step 2 - Regional clues

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/guam/gu_coverage.png)](https://www.plonkit.net/images/guam/gu_coverage.png)

Coverage in Guam is fairly limited, particularly in the southern half, where there is almost no coverage inland.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/guam/gu_hillysouth.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/542NLfJXbMdfNzQE9)

**Hills** are mostly found on the southern half of the island.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/guam/gu_flatnorth.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/FTkRJj1AEnDoWnwP8)

Similarly, the northern half of the island is mostly **flat**.

### Step 3 - Spotlight

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/guam/Apra_View_Hint.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/AKPVwkAEFB5Wjyes6)

Some Signposts near the town of Apra View are coloured **yellow**.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/guam/gu_leopalace.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/mXQvkj25qFMZJRJR8)

LeoPalace Resort can be recognised by the white, resort buildings with **orange tiled roofs**, which looks superficially similar to tiled roofs found on [Java](https://goo.gl/maps/E6P4UxfQsHz8wZoe7).
