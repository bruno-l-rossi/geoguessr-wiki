### Step 1 - Identifying Saint Pierre and Miquelon

![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/saint-pierre-and-miquelon/0_summary_stpm.png)

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/saint-pierre-and-miquelon/Saint_Pierre_and_Miquelon_in_France.svg.png)](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/49/Saint_Pierre_and_Miquelon_in_France.svg/1920px-Saint_Pierre_and_Miquelon_in_France.svg.png)

Saint Pierre and Miquelon, officially the Territorial Collectivity of Saint-Pierre and Miquelon, is an overseas department and region of France, south of Newfoundland in Canada. As such, for country streaks purposes, it is **counted as France**.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/saint-pierre-and-miquelon/pm_licenceplate.png)](https://www.plonkit.net/images/saint-pierre-and-miquelon/pm_licenceplate.png)

Licence plates can be either white or yellow and either short or long. They all have a blue EU strip however, and the white plates will also have a blue strip on the right, as is custom in France. Vehicles may or may not have front plates.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/saint-pierre-and-miquelon/pm_trekker.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/V2GRbCTfmTVgBk1V8)

All coverage was taken with a **Generation 3 trekker camera**.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/saint-pierre-and-miquelon/pm_architecture.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/hiNLV117tvmdphZE6)

Most houses are made of wooden panels with a **boxy look** and often **vivid colours**.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/saint-pierre-and-miquelon/pm_landscape.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/ib3aZzykXYTwCXC48)

The landscape mainly consists of flat or rolling **grasslands**. The coast will be visible more often than not.

### Step 2 - Regional clues

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/saint-pierre-and-miquelon/pm_coverage.png)](https://www.plonkit.net/images/saint-pierre-and-miquelon/pm_coverage.png)

Coverage is fairly limited, being only found in the eastern parts of Saint-Pierre as well as at the northern tip of Miquelon-Langlade.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/saint-pierre-and-miquelon/Miquelon_Langlade_Town.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/JPErMrsLx9ukv7ms7)

Miquelon-Langlade is a small town in the north of St Pierre. It can be recognized by the houses with timber cladding painted in **bold, vivid colours**. [A shadow of the ATV](https://goo.gl/maps/QKtCCJyEA33ukUTw9) used to cover this town can also be seen.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/saint-pierre-and-miquelon/St_Pierre_Town.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/3iu4tyYcwYvSnnrS8)

Saint-Pierre is the capital and largest town. The coverage in this town is made with a **sidewalk trekker**. Larger buildings and more cars can be seen, with a more populated feel compared to Miquelon-Langlade.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/saint-pierre-and-miquelon/Ile_aux_Marins.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/33dErMGJE2atHxj3A)

A slightly **hilly, grass** y landscape with boxy houses spaced apart can be seen on Île-aux-Marins.

### Step 3 - Spotlight

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/saint-pierre-and-miquelon/Rural_North.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/aeiccedKCiSDMtiNA)

This rural trekker in the far north can be recognized by the **rolling, grassy** landscape. The trekker can also be seen walking on a wooden path, with trees on either side.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/saint-pierre-and-miquelon/Saint_Pierre_Island_Rural.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/HS4posLRKnSuLpe67)

The rural trekker on Saint Pierre island has a more **rocky** landscape. The trekker also walks on a path with trees on both sides.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/saint-pierre-and-miquelon/Boat.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/hXdyVUU1TEvrCY5D6)

This **boat** **trekker** between Saint Pierre Island and Île-aux-Marins can be recognized by this man wearing a grey and black sweater.
