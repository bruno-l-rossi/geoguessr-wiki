### Step 1 - Identifying Lebanon

![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/lebanon/0_Shitcam_Summary_Graphic.png)

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/lebanon/lb_licenceplates.png)](https://www.plonkit.net/images/lebanon/lb_licenceplates.png)

Standard Lebanese licence plates are either long with a blue strip on the left, similar to most licence plates in Europe, or **short** with a similar blue strip. Licence plates on taxis are notably **deep red**. You may also find licence plates with [other colours](https://goo.gl/maps/oQN6Fvb8y2GSypoC6).

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/lebanon/lb_arabic.png)](https://www.plonkit.net/images/lebanon/lb_arabic.png)

The official language of Lebanon is **Arabic**, however [English](https://goo.gl/maps/wAohfpzv2g5ayJyy7) and [French](https://goo.gl/maps/JJ7RJ4cZ246BJksc9) are also common to see.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/lebanon/lb_shitcam.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/iFa9EfF7U672jiV67)

Lebanon is currently the only Middle Eastern country that has official **shitcam coverage**.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/lebanon/lb_trekker.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/ivF5SCCKd4r3uK4o9)

Apart from the shitcam, all of the Lebanese coverage was taken with a **Generation 3 trekker**.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/lebanon/lb_landscape.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/XZ9yWgSmuLSArWUk6)

Lebanon has a **Mediterranean landscape**, with a lot of hills and rocky cliffs.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/lebanon/Lebanon_Poles.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/tAWXTZWp4SjQyEis6)

Lebanon uses a variety of **metallic mesh poles**, which are sometimes **painted bright yellow**. Generic-looking wooden poles are common too.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/lebanon/lb_sandstonebricks.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/iTXPFrsoaiso5gGp8)

While several types of architecture can be found on the Lebanese coverage, it is worth mentioning that many buildings are made of **sandstone bricks**.

NOTE: These bricks often look very similar to [Jerusalem stone](https://www.google.com/maps/@31.8956347,35.198306,3a,75y,245.85h,124.16t/data=!3m6!1e1!3m4!1s21rJNnw9rW4MmPpgX4NHNw!2e0!7i13312!8i6656?entry=ttu), commonly found in Israel and the West Bank.

### Step 2 - Region-specific clues

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/lebanon/Lebanon_Coverage_Map.png)](https://www.plonkit.net/images/lebanon/Lebanon_Coverage_Map.png)

Most of the coverage is concentrated near the west coast, north of Beirut.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/lebanon/Lebanon_East_Flat_Hint.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/cP5JVW85DD8rDBmC9)

The region around Zahle has flat agricultural fields, with mountains visible to the northwest.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/lebanon/Lebanon_Snow_Hint.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/ZKYT3yDDZJ8AwsmY6#extra%5Btags%5D=Snow)

Coverage where **snow** is visible on mountain tops, or on the ground, is found in the northeastern mountains, east of Tripoli.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/lebanon/Lebanon_Very_Flat_Hint.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/KmMWgXBCQ59MyiH48#extra%5Btags%5D=Greenhouse)

Northern Lebanon is **very flat** and **agricultural**. You might see the mountains to the southeast.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/lebanon/Lebanon_Black_Car_Hint.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/1Gj4Tn5wvNjqU7HbA)

A visible black car can be found in and around Zahle.

### Step 3 - Gen 3 Trekkers

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/lebanon/lb_trekkercoverage.png)](https://www.plonkit.net/images/lebanon/lb_trekkercoverage.png)

The Generation 3 trekker coverage in Lebanon is **extremely limited**, mostly being found around tourist attractions, university campuses and trails. Note that due to the large number of distinct trekkers, this guide will only cover those that are considered pinpointable.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/lebanon/lb_beirut.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/Yz5EHxGwfPh1SKQC7)

Beirut is the capital of Lebanon and the only covered city with many modern **high-rise buildings**. While the city might not be clearly visible from all parts of the coverage, you will almost always be able to see urban [buildings](https://goo.gl/maps/QbcXbyAKVZ5qwiFW6) or [infrastructure](https://goo.gl/maps/uLp9kfysBxmAr5Db9) in the background.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/lebanon/lb_beirutcoast.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/Fm7ipn8TSJfYkyiy7)

Coverage was taken along parts of the promenade in Beirut, with a grey brick pavement and a large number of fan palms.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/lebanon/lb_usj.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/noFmNwbewTPxPnS59)

Université Saint Joseph, southeast of central Beirut can be recognised by the old, **grand-looking** **sandstone building**.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/lebanon/lb_beirutarabuni.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/QFruHNnBUSwieDGy7)

Campus Debbieh of the Beirut Arab University consists of somewhat sparsely built large **modern sandstone buildings** on top of a forested hill. [The ocean](https://goo.gl/maps/JABVUYU4L9Zztrxm8) can typically be seen far away to the west.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/lebanon/lb_liu.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/y7vp4LyNC198MMyM9)

The Lebanese International University (LIU) can be recognised by the many large stone buildings having unpainted bottom floors while the floors above are painted beige.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/lebanon/lb_balamanduni.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/vr4q4NubrXySzSY78)

The University of Balamand has a lot of modern **white painted buildings**. The buildings often have a very cubic look.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/lebanon/lb_lebaneseamericanuni.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/amU8qk9x4ZRvfs6c7)

The Lebanese American University, east of Byblos, has a very **urban look**, with a lot of construction work and large modern buildings.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/lebanon/lb_byblos.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/ox8Mdbp1BwfMZbS96)

The ancient city of Byblos is one of the oldest cities in the world and can be recognised by the many **old historical buildings**, almost all built out of the same type of unpainted stone bricks.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/lebanon/lb_bsharricanyon.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/AJc5vp5hPnoYZa7a7)

The trekker near Bsharri can be recognised by the **large canyon** to the south.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/lebanon/lb_baalbek.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/SchY7afwE5gGjyWCA)

In Baalbek you can find a large complex of **Roman ruins**.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/lebanon/lb_mseilhafort.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/GZQ7WY6ykS6cFW1v5)

The Mseilha fortress can be found southwest of Tripoli and is built on a monolithic limestone rock.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/lebanon/lb_romanroad.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/NYq3MzkuWBN9kEnR7)

The only **rural coastal road** with trekker coverage in Lebanon can be found southwest of Tripoli.
