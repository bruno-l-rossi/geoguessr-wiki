### Step 1 - Identifying Turkey

![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/turkey/0_tr_summary_281_29.png)

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/turkey/tr_licenceplate.png)](https://www.plonkit.net/images/turkey/tr_licenceplate.png)

Turkey uses the same licence plates as most of Europe: they are long and white, with a **blue strip** on the left.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/turkey/tr_language.png)](https://www.plonkit.net/images/turkey/tr_language.png)

The Turkish language has a fairly unique look. It uses the following special characters:

- Ç ç and Ş ş with a [cedilla](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cedilla) (“hook” below the letter).
- Ö ö and Ü ü with an [umlaut](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Umlaut_\(diacritic\)) (two dots).
- Ğ ğ with a [breve](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Breve).
- Dotless I ı. This letter in particular is noteworthy, as it is essentially unique to Turkish. In case you are confused: this is similar to the letter i, but without a dot when written in lowercase.
- Capital İ with a dot.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/turkey/tr_bollard.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/vJsmH4MHfe8u57B98)

Turkish **bollards** have a simple design: rectangular, plain white, and with a red reflector on the front. They are thin when seen from the side.

NOTE: Romania has similar bollards. However, bollards are relatively rare in Romania.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/turkey/tr_roadlines.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/Y1jTNnVnJ6gTWr9n6)

The colour of Turkish road lines is somewhat inconsistent. Most roads simply have white road lines. However, it is not uncommon to see yellow road lines. These roads can have [fully yellow lines](https://goo.gl/maps/NDYQg17SRma1M8VY9), or a [combination of yellow and white lines](https://goo.gl/maps/kVzg32BeNHnxXNhb8).

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/turkey/tr_saltandpepper.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/vpzLcfJkyY77wkui8)

Many roads have pavement with a grainy “ **salt and pepper** ” texture.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/turkey/tr_divided.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/uG7eY44ViTznRigq9)

**Divided highways** are extremely common, especially compared to other Mediterranean countries.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/turkey/tr_chevron.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/YQnd7NLQpnNw44q36)

Turkish chevrons are typically **red on white**. Beware that there are some other [regional variants](https://goo.gl/maps/c53U4ydsoRYVxGhk9).

Usually, the tip of the arrow does not touch the edge of the chevron. Some chevrons have a [neon yellow border](https://goo.gl/maps/h52NSmYxDJA77zVD8).

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/turkey/tr_chevron2.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/rb6kRgiqproQUWFE6)

On intersections, you can often find this distinctive **black and yellow** chevron, with multiple arrows that point upward.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/turkey/tr_chevron1.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/Ai1JLo8zhvg4NCKF7)

Sometimes, you can see these **narrow chevrons** that have a red and white colour scheme.

NOTE: Almost identical chevrons can be found in [South Africa](https://www.google.com/maps/@-31.9255787,26.9327084,3a,15y,260.14h,77.09t/data=!3m7!1e1!3m5!1safeEbQeUOh2C-t3z0hfx6Q!2e0!5s20230401T000000!7i16384!8i8192?entry=ttu).

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/turkey/tr_directionalsigns.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/W4m2vDDmwBdvxCAV9)

Direction signs use a very distinct font that can often be recognised by the noticeably **wide letter spaces**.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/turkey/tr_snowpole.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/cupTkRgXiDKx2LJt7)

Snow poles usually feature a colour sequence of black-orange-black-white from top to bottom, with small red reflectors.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/turkey/tr_dur.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/HUDf91iQqjFM8xHa8)

Stop signs have the word “ **DUR** ” written on them. This is unique to Turkey.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/turkey/tr_guardrails.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/hXWYWvF8DxS5Am816)

Turkey uses both main types of guardrails:

- A-type guardrails, which have **rounded** edges.
- B-type guardrails, which have **square** edges.

While most Mediterranean countries use the A-type, B-type can help solve some tough 50-50s. Furthermore, seeing [both types](https://www.google.com/maps/@38.7634071,36.9889726,3a,75y,279.13h,72.18t/data=!3m6!1e1!3m4!1s-lWzgc6PumTFgOVCosSYkQ!2e0!7i16384!8i8192?entry=ttu) will exclude all southern European and Middle Eastern countries, other than Montenegro and Israel & the West Bank. For a full overview of which guardrails are found in which European country, see [this infographic](https://www.plonkit.net/images/turkey/europeguardrail.png).

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/turkey/tr_pole.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/i4r3FcX5N9hDQzgs6)

Metal poles, either in the shape of a **ladder** or with a **mesh** pattern, are extremely common in Turkey.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/turkey/tr_gen3noantenna.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/gpEXoAqh8raW3xar7)

In the **Generation 3** coverage, the Google car **never has an antenna**.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/turkey/tr_gen4car.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/GsRJayyumGYmmju3A)

The **Generation 4** coverage in Turkey typically features a **white Google car**. Usually, only a tiny sliver of the back of the car is visible. The car can have a short antenna, or [no antenna](https://goo.gl/maps/cDZNhkcAjdTX5zw89) at all.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/turkey/tr_pickuptruck.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/Ebd2AVU36nUc1NNF9)

A **grey pickup truck** can be found in some Generation 4 coverage.

NOTE: Similar pickup trucks can also be found in [Senegal](https://maps.app.goo.gl/qVLNzZSjP3uTWXcg8) and [Kenya](https://maps.app.goo.gl/ggiEBc94DWL7BQwp7).

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/turkey/Turkey_Smallcam.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/e5EJ3feR9vWbaYz38)

Turkey also has Generation 4 “smallcam” coverage, which is mounted lower than regular Generation 4 and has a relatively big circular blur with a small protrusion at the front. The blur can also be fully transparent, making it harder to spot.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/turkey/tr_landscape.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/eXQf5WpzjEQE2dh29)

The Turkish landscape ranges from arid in the [southeast](https://goo.gl/maps/LjNc6hYmCyfbay51A) to a Mediterranean landscape in the [southwest](https://goo.gl/maps/gEzXWMtN6TYYx4GG7). In the [north](https://goo.gl/maps/odAj9owBhLGwSmBH7) you will often find a more forested and lush landscape. The most typical landscape is semi-arid, either rolling or flat, with vast agricultural fields, containing very limited vegetation other than crops.

Beware that Turkey is a very naturally diverse country with many landscapes that are not mentioned in this tip.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/turkey/tr_apartmentblocks.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/DWQkLUfxdgnn5fUdA)

Tall and somewhat square **apartment blocks** are very common in Turkey.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/turkey/tr_mosque.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/s8NDvpw9Go8Ezbvx5)

Turkey is a muslim country, and as such mosques can be found in most towns. In particular, tall [**minarets**](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Minaret) are extremely common and a good clue for the country.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/turkey/tr_brickpavement.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/m6zfUb5Lx2osZgxg9)

**Brick pavement** is very common in urban areas, and the bricks will typically have the shape shown in the image.

### Step 2 - Region-specific clues

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/turkey/tr_areacodes_nobg.png)](https://www.plonkit.net/images/turkey/tr_areacodes_nobg.png)

Turkish **area codes** follow a fairly easy to learn pattern and can be very useful to get the general region. Most notably, the first digit of area codes are ordered from 2 in the west to 4 in the east.

NOTE: Phone codes starting with 5 are not regional.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/turkey/tr_droads.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/UtnsVceNUnyXTTkS8)

Turkey has three main types of numbered roads. The largest type are the [**O-roads**](https://goo.gl/maps/WeocadhrmCAqWDWc6), which on kilometre markers feature an O followed by a hyphen and the road number. The second largest type are the **D-roads**, which omit the “D” on kilometre markers and instead only feature a 3-digit road number, followed by the road section number on the top and the kilometre at the bottom. The section number will generally increase to the southeast.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/turkey/tr_provinceroads.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/tkqjw6hayPez3ff96)

The third type of roads are provincial roads. While these generally aren’t labelled on the map, they can still be used to regionguess since the kilometre markers feature the [**provincial code**](https://sites.google.com/site/roadnumberingsystems/home/countries/turkey?authuser=0) as the first two digits on the sign. These codes generally increase alphabetically from 01 to 76 with some newer regions being unordered. Shown in the image is a road from the Afyon Province (03).

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/turkey/turkeytruckdistibution_credit_to_vols_jezuz.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/fRi7xv5DFxRNGQqm8)

Generation 4 **truck coverage** is mainly found in the far east of the country and along the southern coast, with smaller pockets in the west and around Kastamonu.

For a more detailed look, see this [document](https://docs.google.com/document/d/1VJQSEVVZxJHJebLwgQ2aEoS6ML0MENral7g-IiCnRlg/).

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/turkey/tr_northlandscape.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/WLpMbKsowgbihvFRA)

Northern Turkey tends to be significantly **lusher** and **more forested** than the rest of the country.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/turkey/tr_northeast.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/3bJjrEbqhmAYbatH8)

In the northeast you will find **uncultivated grasslands** in valleys surrounded by round hills. Trees are very limited in the region. In Generation 3 the area has an extremely distinct look, with deep green grass covering the hills. It is significantly more difficult to recognise in Generation 4, where the grass has a more arid look.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/turkey/tr_pines.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/9ovS9sPtcHdmigWy5)

**Turkish pines** ([pinus brutia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pinus_brutia)) are mainly found near the Mediterranean coast.

NOTE: Turkish pines can appear very similar to [Aleppo pines](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pinus_halepensis), which are uncommon in Turkey, but are often found in [other Mediterranean countries](https://www.google.com/maps/@41.0385689,0.4753459,3a,48.5y,144.08h,92.16t/data=!3m6!1e1!3m4!1shQfy4LKJz9mGqNx8fa89MA!2e0!7i13312!8i6656?entry=ttu).

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/turkey/tr_rizetea.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/QZpfT8iBvzxonRwu6)

**Tea plantations** are common in the province of Rize.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/turkey/tr_olives.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/sZnqtdNofT1jUjXc9)

**Olive trees** are mainly found on the Mediterranean coast.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/turkey/tr_sunflowers.png)](https://www.google.com/maps/@40.1570685,34.6725306,3a,47.7y,268.05h,80.12t,0.12r/data=!3m6!1e1!3m4!1s8JwvYfrDtSsWI3zM6RnQlw!2e0!7i16384!8i8192?entry=ttu)

**Sunflowers** are mainly found in northwestern Turkey.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/turkey/tr_wine.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/MSDrzGXe7RLViLLa8)

A majority of all **vineyards** in Turkey can be found in a region east of Izmir.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/turkey/tr_cotton.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/H4awdN1QhhCmXmao6)

**Cotton** is mainly found in the region surrounding the city of Şanlıurfa.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/turkey/tr_lakevan.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/cEKtbAFE1eEu9qmx9)

Lake Van is by far the biggest lake in Turkey and has a semi-arid landscape, being surrounded by dry grassy hills and occasional trees and [agricultural fields](https://goo.gl/maps/LCwckUWMg8zV8yrX7) near the shore.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/turkey/tr_blackstones.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/KBat19kwZeAmfeEAA)

Dark, **volcanic rocks** are commonly found between the cities of Şanlıurfa and Diyarbakır.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/turkey/tr_aridrockyvalleys.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/LpZkGx243n5obPmF9)

River valleys surrounded by steep, **barren and rocky cliff sides** can be found between the cities of Erzurum and Artvin and in the [southeast](https://goo.gl/maps/zDhSPCWVAnGq3Ms18) near the city of Hakkâri.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/turkey/tr_tiledroofs.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/M1fFRrJ1wT7CMqcj6)

**Tiled roofs** are mostly found in the western two thirds of the country.

Note that for this and the following roof-related tips to be reliable, the majority of all visible roofs should preferably be of that type.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/turkey/tr_flatroofs.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/YtLZLBGAdaPt8nda7)

**Flat roofs** are mostly found in the southeast and to a lesser extent along the south coast.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/turkey/tr_metalroofs.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/RyUM1gqESkYEDiMC8)

**Metal roofs** are most common in the east.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/turkey/tr_pyramidmetalroof.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/3WiR6NCdykeqLtRh7)

Sheet metal roofs shaped like **low pyramids** are mainly found far east, particularly in the general area surrounding Lake Van.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/turkey/tr_nevsehirbrick.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/f8ccBYkMocaG3YTd6)

Many buildings in the Nevşehir Province are built by large, light and unevenly coloured, **sandstone bricks**.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/turkey/tr_chevronyellowonblack.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/c53U4ydsoRYVxGhk9)

**Yellow on black chevrons** are mainly found in and around the Balıkesir Province in the west and in the [Kars Province](https://www.google.com/maps/@40.7194663,43.1857553,3a,15y,58.93h,86.15t/data=!3m6!1e1!3m4!1scyws6VGByT2Y02WeHnA4dw!2e0!7i16384!8i8192?entry=ttu) in the far east. Beware that you may very rarely find them [elsewhere](https://goo.gl/maps/bQYsRcd5eqHj3x1h7).

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/turkey/tr_chevronblackonyellow.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/dnVaEoQ2WCfTGuZD6)

**Black on yellow chevrons** are mainly found north of Izmir.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/turkey/tr_eastdivider.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/vgE5NejjjYyc8UR29)

Four-lane divided highways with **no divider**, other than double middle road lines, are almost exclusively found in the eastern half of the country.

Beware that divided highways with [clear dividers](https://goo.gl/maps/SLzhkGpReMShmtDt9), such as guardrails, can still be found in the east.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/turkey/tr_watertankvertical.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/9VnQL1ZgJ5vAUyq69)

**Vertical water tanks** can be found in most of Turkey, but are notably uncommon along the south and west coast.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/turkey/tr_watertankhorizontalvertical.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/yswVLscbSMSot5sT6)

Horizontal water tanks placed **on top of** vertical water tanks are primarily found in a line following the south coast and going up to Elâzığ.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/turkey/tr_watertanksquare.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/uzbmLqiAu5y7VSJy9)

**Square water tanks** with angled corners are found in the south, approximately from İskenderun to Sanliurfa.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/turkey/tr_watertanksphere.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/vN2VozWbCXPbUvUPA)

White **spherical water tanks** can be found in the southeast, mainly south of Batman.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/turkey/Turkey%20European%20Pole%20Plates.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/gL2TRPecD7ggyqjj7)

In the European part of Turkey, except Istanbul, utility poles, and other electrical infrastructure, will have a 6 digit code, which is often seen on a small pole plate under the yellow electricity warning plate.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/turkey/tr_ankarastreetsign.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/LuJK3ANtPMW1kxwx9)

Street signs in the Ankara Province are blue with a small **round top**. Similar street signs can very rarely be found in [Giresun](https://goo.gl/maps/aVtMnDJsqJ1ms4jf6).

Beware that some [newer street signs](https://www.google.com/maps/@39.9941773,32.8704415,3a,15y,27.52h,88.69t/data=!3m7!1e1!3m5!1sHNDiTJyKaZv36GFlgPI6wg!2e0!5s20221101T000000!7i16384!8i8192?entry=ttu) are red.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/turkey/tr_izmirstreetsign.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/S1toMRrMMXXkA4iC6)

Street signs in the Izmir Province are dark blue, noticeably **bulge outwards** and very commonly have the **emblem** of Izmir in white at the top of the sign.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/turkey/tr_greenhouses.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/5epDcoP5q6JLsdwD6)

If you find yourself surrounded by **greenhouses**, you are likely to be along the south coast west of Adana.

### Step 3 - Spotlight

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/turkey/tr_greekarchitecture.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/sTp8L4cY2wKvk3CK9)

Around the city of Bodrum you will commonly find this type of **whitewashed buildings** with flat roofs, often combined with [blue details](https://goo.gl/maps/jcmMEtAFzgkLEf466) or [unpainted stone walls](https://goo.gl/maps/GvWo5wWdFEorZkaP8).

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/turkey/tr_g_C3_B6reme.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/ucRqWEjyw7twJSVa9)

Göreme, west of Kayseri, can be recognised by the tall **sandstone monoliths** with ancient dwellings carved into them.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/turkey/tr_mountararat.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/NFxmbn1T6tfaVHZW7)

[Mount Ararat](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mount_Ararat), labelled as Ağrı Dağı on Google Maps, is a dormant volcano in far eastern Turkey and the **tallest mountain** in the country. It is often seen together with Küçük Ağrı, a smaller volcano to the southeast.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/turkey/tr_banana.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/CEzMawbJt4z1xQRG9)

**Banana plants** are found around the city of Alanya.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/turkey/tr_e99.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/1gZeqihSjJw4qrxy5)

Road E99 south of Şanlıurfa is a divided highway which can be recognised by the slightly hazy Generation 4 sunrise coverage combined with the flat agricultural landscape and general south-north road angle.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/turkey/tr_d525.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/yRjxXvLUnmBtDhwA8)

Road D525 south of Söke is a completely straight road going south-south-west to north-north-east with winter Generation 3 coverage. Along the road you will typically find a large amount of reed.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/turkey/tr_lakebeysehir.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/NHzpaqhFs9g1JHSj6)

Along the western shore of Lake Beyşehir and [Lake Eğirdir](https://goo.gl/maps/9R4Hc6UMzMLLTn7g6) you can find this small single lane road with jagged, exposed rock and [Turkish pines](https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pinus_brutia) to the west. These two can be differentiated by the road west of Lake Eğirdir having a middle road line.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/turkey/tr_bozcaadasign.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/X6WmZLDEPY6esYT16)

These light blue and grey **touristic signs** which are either arrow shaped or rectangular with a half circle on the top can be found on the island of Bozcaada.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/turkey/tr_streetsignistanbul.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/zTReodSx4dEU2Hhz5)

Red street signs with a coloured bottom strip are found in several cities. In the bottom right corner, you can find a white arrow and some numbers. If this arrow is pointing in **both directions** and has a **single arrowhead**, rather than [double](https://goo.gl/maps/Fd5tuzksA3NY8YsF7), then you are almost certainly in Istanbul.

Beware that simple arrows pointing in only one direction can be found in both [Istanbul](https://goo.gl/maps/6uFmLFqcEn2bT7vL9) and in [other cities](https://goo.gl/maps/BBU6aeQ2BoBN38YH8). However, if you see such signs in combination with [red house numbers](https://goo.gl/maps/kjNkwByQZ7rNaQXdA), then you should be in Istanbul.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/turkey/tr_streetsigntrabzon.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/r3tSKDy4QtLFoBqq9)

Street signs in Trabzon and [Yalvaç](https://goo.gl/maps/vZmpbacvJeyzbMWc9) are **red** and **oval-shaped**. [House numbers](https://goo.gl/maps/96X34Wg24K87jPG4A) also feature the same design.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/turkey/tr_streetsignbursa.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/pJ5WsrmSv7RBVxp47)

Street signs in Bursa are **green**, and generally have slightly **extruded letters**, meaning the signs are not completely flat.

NOTE: Several other types of infrastructure in Bursa, such as [fences](https://goo.gl/maps/EHzDyAUKLCQySbyB7) and [guardrails](https://goo.gl/maps/nF72j1rxibsT8weR8), may also be coloured green.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/turkey/tr_streetsignbolu.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/J5bS9QhXiWqXWPEQA)

Street signs in Bolu are also **green**, but are **completely flat** and lack any kind of border.

### Step 4 - Maps and resources
