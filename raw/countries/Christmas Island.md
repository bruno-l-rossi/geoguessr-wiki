### Step 1 - Identifying Christmas Island

![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/christmas-island/0_Christmas_Island_Summary_Graphic.png)

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/christmas-island/Christmas_Island_in_its_region.png)](https://www.plonkit.net/images/christmas-island/Christmas_Island_in_its_region.png)

Christmas Island is a small **island territory** south-southwest of Java. It is an Australian external territory, so for country streaks purposes it is counted as part of Australia.

NOTE: Do not get its location confused with the Cocos (Keeling) Islands, which are another Australian territory a little west of Christmas Island.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/christmas-island/Christmas-Island-car-meta.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/ZE5rKdbucuL7C57LA)

Christmas Island has two types of coverage: car coverage and a couple of trekkers.

The car coverage was done with a unique Google car: a **silver pickup truck**, with the bed of the truck visible. Usually, this is all you need to identify Christmas Island.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/christmas-island/Christmas-island-landscape.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/W254cWGU8jD1iHZw7)

Most of Christmas Island is covered by dense **tropical forest**. Some unpaved roads feature bright, white sandy soil. A small part of the coverage is in built-up areas. And in some places, [palms](https://goo.gl/maps/wzh7z1FVTpBp32RM6) can be seen.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/christmas-island/Christmas-Island-bollard.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/pYjhx7GagCKCJKMM7)

Christmas Island uses the standard **Australian bollard**. It is a simple white bollard with a red reflector on the front, and a grey reflector on the back.

NOTE: In general, road infrastructure metas are similar to those found on mainland Australia, such as [speed limit signs](https://goo.gl/maps/bMWYvWn1yBfSiWs29), [green sign backgrounds](https://goo.gl/maps/EDq64mntxxcKBnrk7), etc.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/christmas-island/Christmas_Island_License_Plate.png)](https://www.plonkit.net/images/christmas-island/Christmas_Island_License_Plate.png)

Christmas Island has **yellow** licence plates.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/christmas-island/Christmas-Island-rural-coverage.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/rELwvrHpUnRPJBLy7)

While much of the coverage is on regular roads, the Google car also drove on some very **rural trails**.

### Step 2 - Regional clues

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/christmas-island/Christmas-island-urban-areas.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/T9vCR8AVcYJZTFTJ9)

The **built-up areas** are in the northeast. The rest of the island is mostly covered by dense forest.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/christmas-island/Boat_Trekker.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/F9Mw5wAJV8oJCM76A)

There is a **boat trekker** near the north coast of the island. Some of the boat’s white-coloured deck pokes through the blur. Much of the trekker closely hugs the coast; here you can see the steep, rocky coastline of Christmas Island.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/christmas-island/Trail_Trekkers.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/3kXgsWxbV3rY6FCT9)

There is an abundance of smaller **trail trekkers** all over the island. These are mostly surrounded by very dense foliage, but occasionally you can encounter interesting sights like [beaches](https://goo.gl/maps/MTioJbLvuheFE9ib9) and [lookouts](https://goo.gl/maps/pnpihnNe9PLUBKvT9).

NOTE: [Click here for a larger map of their locations.](https://www.plonkit.net/images/christmas-island/Christmas_island_trekkers_map3.png)

### Step 3 - Spotlight

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/christmas-island/Christmas-Island-Jin-Pantai-road.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/hiEA1Xs2r28wDxTz5)

An exception to the road coverage mostly having car meta is the coastal **Jln Pantai road**, where a regular trekker was used. On this road, you will not see the back of the car, just a trekker blur.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/christmas-island/Christmas-Island-cranes.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/jjT34gD15Pvxw6c4A)

The harbour area is recognizable by the **large cranes** that are visible from many vantage points.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/christmas-island/Christmas-Island-golf-course.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/Z24nZ8a3rHKwEQMC6)

This **golf course trekker** is on the northeast coast. In some places, the Google car also drove on this golf course.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/christmas-island/Christmas-Island-red-crabs.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/mtw9YbvqXt3znhfz8)

Christmas Island is famous for its **red crabs**. Near Ethel Beach, you can find a trekker walking among hundreds of these crabs. Another smaller trekker where you can see crabs is [near The Dales](https://goo.gl/maps/deYXgd8DDC3RfBbd6), on the West Coast. Throughout the island, you can find [references](https://goo.gl/maps/jhXdg6Yp6Yo9LpMA9) on signs to these crabs.
