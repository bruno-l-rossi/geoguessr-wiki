### Step 1 - Identifying Northern Mariana Islands

![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/northern-mariana-islands/1_nmi_summary.png)

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/northern-mariana-islands/nmi_location.png)](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f9/Northern_Mariana_Islands_in_Oceania_%28-mini_map_-rivers%29.svg/2388px-Northern_Mariana_Islands_in_Oceania_%28-mini_map_-rivers%29.svg.png)

The Northern Mariana Islands is a United States overseas territory. As such, it is counted as **the US** for country streaks purposes.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/northern-mariana-islands/nmi_car.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/e1TVSUpRgtRetpM26)

The entire Google car is blurred, however an antenna at the front right can be seen. The antenna is also **noticeably closer** to the camera than in the Guam Google car.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/northern-mariana-islands/nmi_pole.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/WcWY9Maj3jL19JjD9)

Northern Mariana Islands have **wooden poles**, frequently with a crossbar at the top.

NOTE: Guam typically has concrete poles.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/northern-mariana-islands/streetsign.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/3E6PHtcJLSiYhBBMA)

The Northern Mariana Islands uses **blue** street signs, unlike Guam, which use green street signs.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/northern-mariana-islands/nmi_landscape.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/iqpm75XrCW6PDLw9A)

Northern Mariana Islands have a **tropical climate** with a lot of dense and somewhat low vegetation.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/northern-mariana-islands/nmi_redflower.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/tnw3drhwSREqtCtc6)

Royal Poinciana, Flamboyant Tree or Flame Tree ([Delonix Regia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Delonix_regia)) is the official tree of the Northern Mariana Islands and very common to see in the territory. Its **wide crown** with **bright red flowers** is easy to recognize.

NOTE: This tree is not common in Guam.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/northern-mariana-islands/nmi_reflectorspam.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/UWyusyAu8qRihpKg9)

Both Northern Mariana Islands and Guam often have a very **large number** of **road reflectors**.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/northern-mariana-islands/nmi_asian.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/Mv91ffZwXQdu9UBe6)

**East Asian languages** such as Japanese, Chinese, Korean, and even Russian are common to see in the Northern Mariana Islands.

NOTE: This is also the case in Guam.

### Step 2 - Regional clues

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/northern-mariana-islands/nmi_coveragemap.png)](https://www.plonkit.net/images/northern-mariana-islands/nmi_coveragemap.png)

The only island with car coverage is the main island of **Saipan**. The islands north of that have no coverage. Tinian and Sinapalu south of Saipan only have **tripod** coverage.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/northern-mariana-islands/nmi_towncentres.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/3ismHgYegwwX6C6a8)

**Dense commercial areas** can be found on the southern half of the west coast.

### Step 3 - Spotlight

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/northern-mariana-islands/nmi_suicidecliff.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/abcbroprRxLYZe9y8)

The **Suicide Cliff** is a steep cliff with a lot of exposed rock near the northern tip of the island.
