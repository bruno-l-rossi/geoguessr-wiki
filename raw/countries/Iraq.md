### Step 1 - Identifying Iraq

![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/iraq/0_iraq_summary.png)

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/iraq/Coverage_location.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/tHuevnuaPPyfP3ST8)

The coverage in Iraq is limited to a single trekker in the **Iraqi National Museum** in Baghdad.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/iraq/Trekker_mounted_on_cart.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/TYRcoYXiDN18npct5)

All of the coverage in Iraq is taken by a **trekker** with a camera similar to **Gen 2** but with distinctly worse image quality and colour. This coverage tends to be overexposed, even becoming blindingly so [at some points](https://goo.gl/maps/YyzKFREKZvguinm16).

NOTE: This camera quality is sometimes referred to as Gen 1.5.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/iraq/Bild_2022-07-01_181801364.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/2yv4UYpvhiUn7LwcA)

The trekker blur has a well-defined, **pointed** **shape**.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/iraq/Bild_2022-07-01_181856327.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/MHY3BKixH2BKhx3p6)

The only piece of coverage outdoors is a small patio with a diagonal walkway. In this patio you can see many **date palms**.

This is the only piece of the coverage that can rarely appear on serious maps.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/iraq/Steeles.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/uN2uxkfFMPMU76np9)

The inside of the museum features statues, steles, a variety of [artefacts](https://goo.gl/maps/WXRcTXkZSMVuChJK6), and a few [back rooms](https://goo.gl/maps/cjfVfUA3sbEWXegF6).
