### Step 1 - Identifying Guatemala

![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/guatemala/0_gt_summary.png)

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/guatemala/gt_licenceplate.png)](https://www.plonkit.net/images/guatemala/gt_licenceplate.png)

Guatemala has short licence plates, which when blurred will typically have either a blue tinge, or a hint of green on the left side.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/guatemala/gt_googlecar.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/D48c6cCqKHfYhQuX9)

This **grey** Street View car with a visible roof rack and side mirrors can be seen on all car coverage in Guatemala.

NOTE: The only other tropical country with a similar car is [Laos](https://maps.app.goo.gl/uMGhM4Th1yi9GsaT9). The car used in the [Dominican Republic](https://goo.gl/maps/Hjv6G6xFYJm1F38i6) may look similar, but has thick black stripes on the bars and the mirrors are not visible.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/guatemala/gt_landscape.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/cwf8vawYn4echrZd6)

Guatemala is a **tropical country**, and is mainly covered by rainforests.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/guatemala/gt_architecture.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/q8y2LD2zs2C8QruE7)

Guatemalan architecture mainly consists of pastel painted stone houses, commonly with rusted sheet metal roofs. It is also somewhat common for buildings to be made out of exposed white brick.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/guatemala/gt_roadlines.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/a9fUCoZEc4nKUxV2A)

Similarly to Mexico, Guatemalan roads generally have solid white outer lines with a **single yellow middle line**. Road lines in Guatemala are generally significantly **thinner** than in [Mexico](https://www.google.com/maps/@15.684137,-92.0520015,3a,67.2y,89.22h,61.96t/data=!3m6!1e1!3m4!1sAWSMGxU5u0okOM9sg-Ah0g!2e0!7i16384!8i8192?entry=ttu).

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/guatemala/gt_alto.png)](https://www.google.com/maps/@14.6255005,-90.5568668,3a,15y,74.14h,81.77t/data=!3m6!1e1!3m4!1smSi97ivNOrtG5_baoEx9YQ!2e0!7i13312!8i6656?entry=ttu)

Like Mexico, Panama and Costa Rica, Guatemala uses the word “ **ALTO** ” on stop signs.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/guatemala/gt_polepaint.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/z3C6xKzkRjpHVv6w6)

In Guatemala you will very commonly see poles painted with either **pink**, **green** or a combination of the two.

### Step 2 - Region-specific clues

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/guatemala/gt_coverage.png)](https://www.plonkit.net/images/guatemala/gt_coverage.png)

Guatemala has a fairly **low amount of coverage**, particularly in the north. Here you can see which roads are covered.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/guatemala/gt_topography.png)](https://www.plonkit.net/images/guatemala/gt_topography.png)

This is a topographic map of Guatemala. Generally speaking, the middle of the country is mountainous, while the south and north are fairly flat.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/guatemala/gt_flatsouth.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/smc84PABGgGm8RvJ9)

The southern regions of Guatemala mainly consist of **completely flat** agricultural land. It is generally more built up than flat areas in the [north](https://www.google.com/maps/@16.6546485,-90.1707062,3a,90y,317.32h,87t/data=!3m6!1e1!3m4!1sk183E0zMqWnu74AJOcvOFQ!2e0!7i13312!8i6656?entry=ttu).

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/guatemala/gt_mountainousmiddle.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/cvr61aTLJurqudZQ6)

The central regions of Guatemala are generally **mountainous**.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/guatemala/gt_semiflatnorth.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/RrMgtXnb9nF7heeFA)

The northern regions of Guatemala are typically **fairly flat**. You will however usually see some hills or a slightly [rolling landscape](https://goo.gl/maps/gn49EV7aAQv94cq6A), rather than the complete flatness of the south.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/guatemala/gt_oilpalm.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/z8fdGA1XkCzwdssE9)

**Oil palm plantations** are primarily found in two general areas: in the southwest and just north of the central mountain range.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/guatemala/gt_greenpoles.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/oiHsWxudChDeaGV39)

**Completely green utility poles** are primarily found in the southeast of the country, but also on a road in the further north in the east and on a small stretch of road around Melchor de Mencos, near the end of the coverage in the far northeast.

These poles also feature small [pole plates](https://goo.gl/maps/sw6JyNjNerwDncwx8), which can further narrow down the road. This will be further explained in the next tip.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/guatemala/gt_poleplate.png)](https://www.plonkit.net/images/guatemala/gt_poleplate.png)

Most green utility poles contain a **white pole plate**, for which the **top two characters** will determine which road you are on. These roads and their corresponding characters are shown in this infographic.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/guatemala/gt_carbarforward.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/qU7Ek9tdd4qyUt159)

In most of the Guatemala coverage, the [top right bar](https://www.plonkit.net/images/guatemala/top_right_bar.png?) will be **facing forward**. This means that you can see [this line](https://www.plonkit.net/images/guatemala/zoomedinbar_line_highlighted.png?) on the side facing forward.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/guatemala/backwards_bar_hint.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/86Gre6p6eGpLLFp46)

The opposite is true in southeastern Guatemala, where the line is facing backwards.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/guatemala/gt_blueropecar.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/gCV9MkB5DKZWTPg28)

In these regions, you can often see a **blue rope** on the bottom right bar.

NOTE: Sometimes it can be [hard](https://maps.app.goo.gl/at4smsdgVk8HbsGaA) or [almost impossible](https://maps.app.goo.gl/HPXi6nrvanWVH3kk8) to see, in which case it might look like a normal backward facing bar.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/guatemala/gt_carbarbackwardnocap.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/aPUqgqM7bmhQnXvn6)

These regions in the south and northeast also have the backwards facing bar. The difference here is that the bottom right bar is **missing** the **thin black cap**, which is present in the other regions.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/guatemala/gt_phonecode2.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/81WKdyb2VyTQfo7R8)

Phone numbers starting with a **2** are unique to Guatemala City.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/guatemala/gt_phonecode6.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/s24s15LqTenyD2jx6)

Similarly, phone numbers starting with a **6** are unique to the Guatemala Department.

NOTE: Phone numbers starting with 7 are found anywhere except for Guatemala Department, and phones starting with 3, 4 or 5 can be found anywhere in the country.

### Step 3 - Spotlight

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/guatemala/gt_lagodeatitlan.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/ZZpvQFbfqkoPfNg59)

Lago de Atitlán is by far the **largest lake** in the mountainous regions of Guatemala. It is surrounded by mountains, and some volcanoes.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/guatemala/gt_northofflores.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/SYZ6QEEndt5nL9x68)

A **small dirt road** in the middle of a **dense jungle** can be found north of Tikal, in the far north of the country.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/guatemala/gt_uniquebollardroad.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/4PMTuyHnYbNwXAoK7)

These small **white bollards** with **two black stripes** can be found on road 1 northeast of Quetzaltenango.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/guatemala/gt_pet11northtosouth.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/hBYUhYoECd9YpMzt5)

The PET-11, south of Las Pozas can be recognised by the slightly rolling landscape combined with its completely **north-to-south road angle**.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/guatemala/gt_ca9.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/tcxK6zbKiZ1bweiG8)

The CA9, going between Escuintla and the coast, can be recognised by being a **divided highway** with **concrete pavement**.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/guatemala/gt_tikal.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/msrYgLWStRDy2FXL7)

A trekker featuring several **Mayan temples** can be found just west of the town of Tikal.

It is worth mentioning that while there are other Guatemalan trekkers featuring [Mayan ruins](https://goo.gl/maps/fTti23Fq4zrCGzUE6), this is by far the most prominent and contains the largest and most intact temple structures.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/guatemala/Guatemala_city_stickers.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/fuj99J6ybsPnZsDE6)

These white rectangular stickers that have the word **‘electricista’** written on them are unique to Guatemala City.
