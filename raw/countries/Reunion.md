### Step 1 - Identifying Réunion

![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/reunion/0_summary_reunion.png)

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/reunion/D_C3_A9partement_974_in_France__28zoom_29.svg.png)](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7a/D%C3%A9partement_974_in_France_%28zoom%29.svg/1920px-D%C3%A9partement_974_in_France_%28zoom%29.svg.png)

Réunion, officially the Department of Réunion, is an overseas department and region of France, east of Madagascar in the Indian Ocean. As such, for country streaks purposes, it is **counted as France**.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/reunion/image__281_29.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/5gnLtSY41NNVVNWZA)

Réunion has a Street View car with visible side mirrors, a roof rack, and **black tape** at the end of the **rear right bar**.

NOTE: Make sure not to get this confused with the Ghana car, which has tape on the front right bar.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/reunion/image.png)](https://www.plonkit.net/images/reunion/image.png)

Being a French Department, you will see **French infrastructure**, such as [signs](https://goo.gl/maps/g4Zs8JkqkiWbQYt28), [bollards](https://www.google.com/maps/@-21.2427103,55.3131219,3a,75y,99.88h,80.84t,358.51r/data=!3m6!1e1!3m4!1sfAEf2UYNe56no-OLI1RWoA!2e0!7i13312!8i6656?entry=ttu) and [poles](https://www.google.com/maps/@-21.281482,55.4317286,3a,90y,19.99h,96.64t/data=!3m6!1e1!3m4!1sm6b5qi8oScL85YCcGo5E_g!2e0!7i13312!8i6656?entry=ttu).

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/reunion/General_Landscape.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/6D7LZ65gmsPn3YYt8)

Réunion is a mountainous volcanic island in the Indian Ocean on a similar latitude to Queensland, Australia. Most roads are along the coast, with lush green mountains visible close by. Vegetation consists of [palms](https://goo.gl/maps/NjLXjEScWkmUzNQ17) and [ferns](https://goo.gl/maps/6oLGxZkvfaiTBqZg7), [pine trees](https://goo.gl/maps/J7vpe2wV2kptobfE9) at higher elevation, and [flowering plants](https://goo.gl/maps/zKst3y8NcdYipxtf6) due to the island’s tropical savanna climate.

### Step 2 - Regional and arrondissement-specific clues

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/reunion/Reunion_Car_Coverage_map.jpg)](https://www.plonkit.net/images/reunion/Reunion_Car_Coverage_map.jpg)

Only a handful of roads were covered without a trekker. Learning the general regions with coverage will help you get those extra 10 points.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/reunion/Reunion_Bus_Stops.png)](https://www.plonkit.net/images/reunion/Reunion_Bus_Stops.png)

There are six distinct bus stop designs on Réunion:

- [Yellow](https://goo.gl/maps/q9CtkypqeALhBfrC7) - This is the most common type and can be found all over the island. It is usually accompanied by another bus stop, meaning you can get a good region guess if you see it on its own.
- [L Shaped](https://goo.gl/maps/Yts9EDaePYnjKk1L8) - It can either be white, or an [olive](https://goo.gl/maps/jgR9cBEvaMmKFu8SA) colour. It may also be in the form of a [bus shelter](https://goo.gl/maps/ERRyoQBRSTDCwNPL6). It is almost never accompanied by a yellow bus stop.
- [Pink](https://goo.gl/maps/psuwbrTTAkRmBatTA) - Rarely accompanied by a yellow bus stop.
- [Orange](https://goo.gl/maps/iab3u7VkEDbP1hSb6) - Almost always accompanied by a yellow bus stop.
- [Green](https://goo.gl/maps/kg61UkiurFFoe8hX6) - Almost always accompanied by a yellow bus stop.
- [Red](https://goo.gl/maps/tL9qM1Uzg6ThwBay7) - Rarely accompanied by a yellow bus stop.

This infographic shows where each one can be found. The list above is ranked by how common each one is found.

*You can click on the image to enlarge it.*

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/reunion/Sugarcane.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/n4M58X5gveDDB4Uv6)

**Sugarcane plantations** on Réunion can be found in two regions: the northeast and the southwest. The southwest is generally more built up and densely populated, meaning you will see more buildings or cars around.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/reunion/Cilaos.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/LHdNKQSPkAtC7kTB8)

Cilaos is a town inside a **caldera**. It can be recognized by the **wall** of mountains surrounding the town with **overcast** coverage.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/reunion/Reunion_Blur_Hint.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/N4vNKy2BDwfFgFbt8)

You can spot this **rectangular blur** on the front camera in all the coverage going north of Saint-Louis.

### Step 3 - Spotlight

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/reunion/Coastal_N1.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/Ji1Bk5ZmKSJ772it6)

The northwestern section of the N1 is divided, with a **distinct ridge** to the southeast and the ocean to the northwest.

Note that although a bridge appears on the map, the coverage was taken before the bridge was built, meaning you will not see the bridge.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/reunion/Route_du_Volcan.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/GFdiB9nbVJPF54Bc7)

Route du Volcan can be recognized by the **Martian** landscape. The only vegetation is scattered shrubs and the soil is a distinct **brown-red** colour. These [white stones](https://goo.gl/maps/UFYFzYRav6h75Fge8) lining the road are also a good clue.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/reunion/Rural_Road.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/m4EWBbcsSnBRhLeCA)

La Route Forestière 58 de la Scierie et du Haut des Makes is a **winding dirt track** that can be recognized by the **dense vegetation** surrounding it, particularly pine trees and eucalyptus. The track itself is also dark in colour, with grass growing in the middle of it.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/reunion/Eastern_N2.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/1vJBXBfvwwi8YyB37)

The eastern region of Réunion is the least densely populated, evident by the eastern part of the N2, which is a single carriageway passing through [somewhat dense vegetation on both sides](https://goo.gl/maps/1vJBXBfvwwi8YyB37), or a [vast landscape](https://goo.gl/maps/dCWLTzJzexxtujih9) with little to no trees.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/reunion/City_Bollards.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/BCFWSpgwGXQNRTZG7)

The two largest towns in Réunion can be differentiated by their **bollards**, commonly seen at intersections. Saint Paul uses [black](https://goo.gl/maps/BCFWSpgwGXQNRTZG7) bollards, while Saint Denis uses [green](https://goo.gl/maps/zKaQEWScWPr9oF4z5) bollards, often with white balls on top. Saint Denis is also more built up, being the capital.
