### Step 1 - Identifying San Marino

![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/san-marino/0_rsm.png)

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/san-marino/San_Marino_Monte_Titano.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/vUPiUkLWrGB1KzXC8)

The 739m-tall **Monte Titano** dominates the surrounding landscape and is visible from almost every point in the Republic.

NOTE: Look for the **three towers** to safely identify it.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/san-marino/San_Marino_Landscape.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/1cqVe47FmXH9gp6bA)

The landscape of San Marino largely consists of smooth **agricultural hills**, with many small winding roads. The vegetation is distinctly **Mediterranean**, and normally appears quite lush.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/san-marino/San_Marino_License_Plate.png)](https://www.plonkit.net/images/san-marino/San_Marino_License_Plate.png)

Sammarinese licence plates are **short**, **white** and have **blue lettering** on them.

NOTE: Other European countries will typically have **longer** licence plates, often with one or more **blue strips**.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/san-marino/San_Marino_Italian_infrastructure.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/5VD2pzJLT1RXj8PCA)

Road signs and bollards are **generally identical** to the ones used in Italy.

NOTE: Many road signs give directions to the nearby Italian city of **Rimini**.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/san-marino/San_Marino_Poles.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/we7AJ1yxXcfgqLLz9)

Just like Italy, most utility poles in San Marino are **round** and madeof **concrete.** Wooden poles are also found in the country.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/san-marino/San_Marino_Chevron.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/bM4E51AfrGfBSFaX8)

San Marino is the only country in the world to use **yellow** chevrons with **burgundy red** arrows.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/san-marino/San_Marino_Pedestrian_Crossing.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/JNpdbvUsjLXyrEuW8)

These **elaborate pedestrian crossings**, with painted blue crosswalks and hanging signs, are specific to San Marino.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/san-marino/San_Marino_Trash_Bins.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/Jr3mAXsrTXH3BojV7)

**Lime-green bins** are particularly common in San Marino. In Generation 4 coverage some of them have been replaced by **black** bins.

### Step 2 - Regional clues

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/san-marino/San_Marino_divided_road.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/i8B3TJE5iChF9pWt8)

The Superstrada di San Marino runs from the border village of Dogana to the City of San Marino. It is the **only divided road** in the country, and the lanes are separated by a **metal guardrail**.
