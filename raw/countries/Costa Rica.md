### Step 1 - Identifying Costa Rica

NOTE: Costa Rica has recently received **full** Street View coverage!  
We’re currently updating the guide to reflect these changes.

![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/costa-rica/0_cr_summary.png)

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/costa-rica/cr_trekker.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/jcS7HQRAthfCyUgcA#extra%5Btags%5D=cemetary&extra%5BloadMode%5D=latLng)

The vast majority of coverage in Costa Rica was taken with a **Generation 3 trekker** camera. It can typically be recognised by being closer to the ground than cameras mounted to cars. On urban rounds it can also often be recognised by the trekker being [on sidewalks](https://maps.app.goo.gl/7wh9spuNbbRVVF2H9), rather than in the middle of the roads.

There are also some [official tripods](https://maps.app.goo.gl/xaYnrnMwRHaJmAaT8#extra%5Btags%5D=tripod&extra%5BloadMode%5D=latLng) inside buildings.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/costa-rica/es_language.png)](https://www.plonkit.net/images/costa-rica/es_language.png)

Like in most of Latin America, **Spanish** is the official language of Costa Rica.

NOTE: No other trekker-only country has Spanish as its main language.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/costa-rica/cr_licenceplate.png)](https://www.plonkit.net/images/costa-rica/cr_licenceplate.png)

Costa Rican licence plates are typically short and white, with **blue text**, giving it a light blue tint through the blur.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/costa-rica/cr_pole.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/XpFA91EXpL56yt679)

Utility poles are typically round and made of concrete.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/costa-rica/cr_alto.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/CMR9fZcyaczBtg3o6)

Like Mexico and Guatemala, Costa Rican stop signs feature the word **“ALTO”**.

### Step 2 - Region-specific clues

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/costa-rica/cr_coveragemap.png)](https://www.plonkit.net/images/costa-rica/cr_coveragemap.png)

The coverage in Costa Rica is **very limited**, mainly being found in and around San José and by the northern west coast. There is also coverage on [Cocos Island](https://goo.gl/maps/RUkuKe4mafK2gbss9), located 500 kilometres southwest of the coast, as well as a boat trekker around [Caño Island](https://goo.gl/maps/CabJDTQ4Cox9ycSQ6) by the southern west coast.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/costa-rica/cr_beach.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/9vRH7Wj3xRdMwLXN8#extra%5Btags%5D=Beach&extra%5BloadMode%5D=latLng)

**Beach trekkers** are mostly found on the northern east coast, but can also be found [west of San José](https://maps.app.goo.gl/W1ft4gW7uTFt9zG3A#extra%5Btags%5D=Beach&extra%5BloadMode%5D=latLng) and on [Cocos Island](https://maps.app.goo.gl/gHZvJaSJNKVMQfFL6#extra%5Btags%5D=Cocos+Forest&extra%5Btags%5D=Beach&extra%5BloadMode%5D=latLng).

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/costa-rica/cr_forest.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/TcdqTUHgzJJFLu4M8#extra%5Btags%5D=Puntarenas&extra%5Btags%5D=forest&extra%5BloadMode%5D=latLng)

Most rural **forest trekkers** are found in central Costa Rica. [Cocos Island](https://maps.app.goo.gl/cFS241KcJxpjsFP87#extra%5Btags%5D=Cocos+Forest&extra%5Btags%5D=forest&extra%5BloadMode%5D=latLng) also mainly consists of dense tropical forests.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/costa-rica/cr_urban.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/1bNHwWwtdF9V4KSCA)

**Urban coverage** is mainly found in and around San José and [Cartago](https://maps.app.goo.gl/pJVj27i5NueHCWBC7#extra%5Btags%5D=Cartago&extra%5Btags%5D=aigen&extra%5Btags%5D=urban&extra%5BloadMode%5D=latLng), but also exists in [Liberia](https://maps.app.goo.gl/FZAENyiYieA99yqi7#extra%5Btags%5D=Costa+Rica&extra%5Btags%5D=pinpointable&extra%5Btags%5D=urban&extra%5BloadMode%5D=latLng) and [Filadelfia de Guanacaste](https://maps.app.goo.gl/dphFESXufA8G7wwE7#extra%5Btags%5D=Filadelfia+park&extra%5Btags%5D=urban&extra%5BloadMode%5D=latLng).

### Step 3 - Pinpointable coverage

This section covers all Costa Rican trekkers which are generally considered pinpointable, meaning they may show up in pinpointable world maps such as A Community World or An Official World.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/costa-rica/cr_childrensmuseum.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/DAnBBch9obwHcDjo8)

The Children’s Museum in northern San José can be recognised by its grand **castle-like** exterior.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/costa-rica/cr_nationalmuseum.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/NgzLmnJ27WEVwaqX9)

Around the National Museum, you can find several [wealthy-looking homes](https://maps.app.goo.gl/UPPchESTtJpkyJKr6), as well as a small tower built in a style resembling towers on medieval European fortresses.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/costa-rica/cr_orangebridge.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/aqLU7Cjvm58uzQQ26)

An **orange pedestrian bridge** can be found going over a major highway at Parque de la Paz in southeastern San José.

While there is [coverage](https://maps.app.goo.gl/5jnT7JS5boCUVQTF8) throughout most of the park, you’re not likely to encounter anything but the bridge in a pinpointable map.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/costa-rica/cr_lasabana.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/fHhiqWUnR9kyT2GC7)

La Sabana Park has several [sports facilities](https://maps.app.goo.gl/8WXHQxLkVboobUpB8), including the National Stadium of Costa Rica, located in the western part of the park.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/costa-rica/cr_university.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/wH83WqFxLrod8aZn9)

The University of Costa Rica, in eastern San José, is fairly busy, with a lot of greenery and various medium-sized buildings strewn across the campus.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/costa-rica/cr_palaciodelosdeportes.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/XX9R3zQVz6SuzodY8)

Palacio de los Deportes is a stadium in Heredia, northwest of San José, which can be recognised by its boxy look combined with the red, blue and white colour scheme.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/costa-rica/cr_airport.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/iWgQDBd3tNwkTDgy6)

At the Juan Santamaría International Airport you can find the trekker walking in the middle of a major four-lane road. There is also coverage just [outside the airport terminal](https://maps.app.goo.gl/d9V4x5cF5arGJQvK9).

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/costa-rica/cr_dogs.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/UrMYKhfDzzKMNmnw9)

In Centro Recreativo Bosque de la Hoja, just east of San José de la Montaña, you can see the trekker being followed by **three dogs** through a pine forest.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/costa-rica/cr_losmanantiales.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/2TnzNGipZxnGjVov7)

In Recreational Park Los Manantiales, just southwest of Poás, you can find a road lined with sugarcane plantations and [cornstalk dracaena](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dracaena_fragrans) plants.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/costa-rica/cr_lagunadefraijanes.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/Sr15C7wry1Mjbeaq5#extra%5Btags%5D=Laguna+de+Fraijanes&extra%5Btags%5D=Getable&extra%5Btags%5D=pinpointable&extra%5BloadMode%5D=latLng)

At the entrance to Laguna de Fraijanes Recreational Park you can find a large **orange wooden gate** separating the road from a parking lot. As the name suggests, the park itself is situated around a [small lagoon](https://maps.app.goo.gl/q9T1iTzTioFFpaY48).

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/costa-rica/cr_cartagomunicipalmuseum.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/B2Q8LqScfNSufu2K9)

The Cartago Municipal Museum can be recognised by its **palace-like facade** with a neatly planted garden in front, as well as the railway in the middle of the street in front of the museum.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/costa-rica/cr_ruinsofcartago.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/9VTrJYcX51VBfP3o9)

The **Ruins of Cartago** is the remains of a large catholic church near the centre of the city. The POI is one of the first things to show up when you zoom into the city.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/costa-rica/cr_cartagostadium.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/d9awGpdGnRUhimFt8)

José Rafael “Fello” Meza Ivankovich Stadium in southern Cartago can be recognised by the **dark blue walls** surrounding it.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/costa-rica/cr_instituteoftechnology.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/7knoeXMBNeiRbhgPA#extra%5Btags%5D=Costa+Rica++IoT&extra%5Btags%5D=aigen&extra%5Btags%5D=urban&extra%5BloadMode%5D=latLng)

Costa Rica Institute of Technology can usually be recognised by the **mountain range** which can be seen to the south and east.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/costa-rica/cr_jardinbotanicolankester.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/Bvn5JvHNr8mD8a2v7#extra%5Btags%5D=aigen&extra%5Btags%5D=jardin&extra%5Btags%5D=pinpointable&extra%5Btags%5D=urban&extra%5BloadMode%5D=latLng)

By the entrance of Jardín Botanico Lankester, located southeast of Cartago, you can find a poorly maintained dirt road, surrounded by mountains in the distance, as well as a [sign](https://maps.app.goo.gl/ieKVnVBKyytKZxRw5#extra%5Btags%5D=aigen&extra%5Btags%5D=jardin&extra%5Btags%5D=pinpointable&extra%5Btags%5D=urban&extra%5BloadMode%5D=latLng) with the name of the garden.

Within the garden itself you will find a plethora of different plants, with the [walkways](https://maps.app.goo.gl/ZeyMUPjpFPoufVLk9) having many soft curves, and almost never being straight.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/costa-rica/cr_puntaleona.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/eZWpV41zGCc35zzRA)

The Punta Leona Resort, which can be found by the “Playa Mantas” POI, can be recognised by the **red brick pavement**, with the bricks having the same shape as you’ll commonly find in [Turkey](https://www.google.com/maps/@40.7468922,31.6196471,3a,48.1y,163.7h,64.62t/data=!3m6!1e1!3m4!1sxgSFnYGwZ7tHRph3WXoxAA!2e0!7i16384!8i8192?entry=ttu).

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/costa-rica/cr_liberia.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/y47nt9ocgaz6bVsA6)

The coverage in Liberia can be recognised by a white building with rounded windows having brown wooden window frames. This building can be seen on all coverage in the town.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/costa-rica/cr_filadelfiadeguanacaste.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/cSyd3CRwTXZG3onw9)

Most of the coverage in Filadelfia de Guanacaste was taken on the edge of a park, with a distinct red brick pavement where the bricks are placed in the shape of an “L”.
