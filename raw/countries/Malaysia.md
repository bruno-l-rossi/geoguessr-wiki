### Step 1 - Identifying Malaysia

![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/malaysia/transparent-my_summary_2026.png)

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/malaysia/Malaysia_License_Plate.png)](https://www.plonkit.net/images/malaysia/Malaysia_License_Plate.png)

Malaysian licence plates are **black** and generally have **two** white sections. These are visible even when blurred.

NOTE: Indonesian licence plates are also black, but they will have **three** white sections.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/malaysia/Road_lines.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/xnmKE1q4Nz91xA528)

Malaysia frequently uses **double white** centre lines on its two-lane roads. It is the only Southeast Asian country to do so.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/malaysia/yellow_road_markings.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/CC7S8r8KEEYHF1yY8)

These **thick yellow bars** are also almost exclusive to Malaysia.

NOTE: You can very rarely see similar patterns in [Hong Kong](https://maps.app.goo.gl/PSUAhJVM8bCQUhH36) and the [United Kingdom](https://maps.app.goo.gl/BKn4346rfTwJj3L26).

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/malaysia/Untitled_design__2825_29.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/FR7u5xsdejfe3Qp8A)

Electricity poles in Malaysia often have **black** or **white** stickers on them.

Sometimes you will also find red-and-white or red-and-blue stripes below the black sticker.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/malaysia/Untitled_design__2824_29.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/qn48nvMwybn7vaQP6)

**BERHENTI** stop signs and **BERI LALUAN** yield signs are both unique to Malaysia.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/malaysia/Untitled_design__2823_29.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/VF2uZG1qdY85Aw9Z9)

Malaysian bollards will often have the colours **black**, **white**, **grey** and **red**. Here are three examples of typical Malaysian bollards.

NOTE: There can be multiple varieties of these bollards, but the layout is mostly the same.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/malaysia/directionalsign.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/CeJasroXurz5dwqC7)

This is a typical Malaysian directional sign. **Hexagonal yellow** highway shieldsare unique to Malaysia.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/malaysia/Untitled_design__2830_29.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/w1fXrLcG2AfWexGX7)

Malaysian kilometre markers have a unique **white-and-blue** colour scheme. Note that the hexagon shields are often visible.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/malaysia/my_language.png)](https://www.plonkit.net/images/malaysia/my_language.png)

The **Malay language** uses the Latin script. It is very closely related to Indonesian. These two languages are practically indistinguishable for people who do not speak either.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/malaysia/chinese.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/1yL5UJiPS593xoNh9)

In contrast to neighbouring Indonesia, **Chinese characters** are often seen in Malaysia. In fact, 24.6% of the Malaysian population is of Chinese descent.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/malaysia/signpost.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/9jwtYKkzUYvhPbjN6)

Signposts in Malaysia almost always have **black-and-white** **stripes**.

NOTE: Within Asia, these posts are also found in Sri Lanka, Bhutan, India, Nepal and Bangladesh. Make sure to check other clues before guessing.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/malaysia/kerb.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/w9fDK214KyxXFxMk9)

**Black-and-white** curbs are commonly found in Malaysia.

NOTE: Many other countries in Asia can also have black-and-white curbs.

### Step 2 - Regional and state-specific clues

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/malaysia/my_roads.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/Ex61LZ4fv1TJtjvGA)

Each Malaysian state, excluding the three federal territories, has its own road numbering system with a **unique letter** at the start of the road number.

NOTE: Sabah also has A roads, but only the A2 and A7.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/malaysia/malaysia_state_flags.png)](https://www.plonkit.net/images/malaysia/malaysia_state_flags.png)

**State flags** can be seen with some regularity, so it is worth learning them.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/malaysia/malaysia_postal_codes_new.png)](https://www.plonkit.net/images/malaysia/malaysia_postal_codes_new.png)

Malaysian postal codes are useful to know, since they can be found on most street signs in the country.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/malaysia/MalayBlackStickers.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/8U23grG4MkMxmbEFA)

**Black pole stickers** are most commonly found on the mainland.

NOTE: The black stickers on Borneo only exist in Generation 4 coverage.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/malaysia/TEL_TNB_Johor_Black_Sticker.png)](https://www.google.com/maps/@1.5189436,103.7986263,3a,21.7y,129.61h,79.69t/data=!3m6!1e1!3m4!1s1XGZcblrLaXJUJG5qoZKNQ!2e0!7i13312!8i6656?entry=ttu)

Black stickers with the text ‘ **TEL TNB** ’ at the bottom, are only found in Johor.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/malaysia/White_Sticker.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/9UCQbRAuiaKvLAkW8)

**White pole stickers** are only found on electricity poles on the island of Borneo, in Sarawak.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/malaysia/my_twocircleslamppost.png)](https://www.google.com/maps/@?api=1&fov=17.531467769923246&heading=165.6592265935562&map_action=pano&pano=Dns61o4eRAsfO8ZdTue4rQ&pitch=23.827270433913796&viewpoint=1.53175%2C103.752783)

Lamp posts with **two small circles** on the support are mainly found on mainland Malaysia.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/malaysia/Triangular_Metal_Mainland_Lamp_Post.png)](https://www.google.com/maps/@2.7610997,102.237736,3a,19y,54.94h,131.15t/data=!3m6!1e1!3m4!1sHN4aoVxo9PPndJk4b5QWIQ!2e0!7i13312!8i6656?entry=ttu)

Lamp posts with a **triangular metal support** are mainly found on mainland Malaysia.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/malaysia/Diagonal_Support_Poles.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/ckyTS9EtUATDRKJr7)

Electricity poles on the Borneo side typically have a **2-1 insulator configuration**, and a long **diagonal wooden support**. They are exclusive to the Sarawak region of the island.

NOTE: Indonesian poles can look similar, but they are usually not made of wood, and use smaller or no diagonal supports.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/malaysia/Two_Horizontal_Support_Poles.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/yBUpZzPgr934PqJD7)

Thin metal poles with **two parallel** pole tops are exclusive to the Sabah region of Borneo.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/malaysia/Sarawak_Lamp_Posts.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/XEDwXRdrZESv1NNEA)

This lamp post with a supporting piece of sheet metal resembling a **boomerang** is found in Sarawak.

NOTE: It is more common in cities other than Kuching.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/malaysia/my_malaccabins.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/y8TTpyS2oMP8Hy6h7)

Trash bins with a **logo resembling an ‘S’** are found in the states of Negeri Sembilan, Malacca and Johor.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/malaysia/my_kedahbins.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/6dEbR4DHXgYEepgq5)

Trash bins with a **logo resembling leaves** are found in Perlis and Kedah.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/malaysia/my_kualalumpurbins.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/n3CSZfspZKFSEHpCA)

Trash bins with this logo featuring a **sunny landscape**, saying **‘Alam Flora’** are found in Pahang, Kuala Lumpur and Putrajaya. The logo will occasionally be absent, and it will just say ‘Alam Flora’.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/malaysia/Johor_Street_Sign.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/VXzBV1PLXpR4YRVp9)

**Yellow street name signs** with a black outline are specific to the state of Johor.

NOTE: This **arrow-shaped** street sign, regardless of its colour, is exclusive to Malaysia.

NOTE: Yellow signs are also found in southern [Negeri Sembilan](https://maps.app.goo.gl/HG89SePzMd5WWheo6), however only the tip of the arrow will be black.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/malaysia/Sabah_Street_Signs.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/sPWtadXnL7eMFFwF6)

**Green** street signs that form an arrow shape and have a **yellow hexagonal symbol** with the letters **JKR** on the left side are found in Sabah.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/malaysia/Sabah_Mountains.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/z6PRqQJHLipvL9eu7)

Assuming you are on Borneo: the state of Sabah is **very mountainous**, and much of the coverage is on mountain roads like this one.

NOTE: Sarawak generally has flatter roads. Even if you see mountains, they will usually be at a [distance](https://goo.gl/maps/5VrnWWJCBfNgREaP7).

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/malaysia/Perlis_State_Isolated_Rock_Formations.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/mE7o9R7VqevTMvLd9)

These **isolated rock formations**, rising abruptly from an otherwise flat landscape, can be found near the Thai border in Perlis state.

### Step 3 - Spotlight

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/malaysia/Labuan_blue_bins.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/gM41nteDBtWu4c3H6)

Urban areas in Labuan are dotted with **blue cylindrical containers**.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/malaysia/Sarawak_Gunung_Mulu_National_Park_Google_Car.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/6ZEMJFXZnpo9fB9i6)

This unique **grey car** with a long front antenna can be found in Gunung Mulu National Park, Sarawak.
