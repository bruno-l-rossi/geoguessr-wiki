### Step 1 - Identifying Jersey

![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/jersey/0_Jersey_Infographic.png)

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/jersey/Jersey_in_its_region2.png)](https://www.plonkit.net/images/jersey/Jersey_in_its_region2.png)

Jersey is a self-governing **island territory** in the Channel Islands, located off France’s northwestern coast. It is a British Crown Dependency, so for country streaks purposes it is counted as part of the United Kingdom.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/jersey/Jersey_vibe.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/UwLv9bL7SsuJYZF16)

The island is shaped by a unique mix of British and Norman influences. The many **winding roads** and hedgerows are reminiscent of English country lanes, with **tree-covered** roads being particularly common. However, due to Jersey’s unique geographical position, most coverage features **sunny** weather.

All images were taken by a **Generation 2** camera.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/jersey/Jersey_License_Plate.png)](https://www.plonkit.net/images/jersey/Jersey_License_Plate.png)

Jersey licence plates follow the British model, having a long **white front** and **yellow rear** plate. In addition, you will notice the island’s red coat of arms on the left, as well as the letter ‘J’ at the beginning of all codes in unblurred plates.

NOTE: In contrast to the British mainland, Jersey was never a member of the European Union. This means that local plates do not have a blue strip.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/jersey/UK_style_infrastructure.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/kNsowiMi9Dd68QKt5)

In general, road infrastructure is **similar** to that found in the United Kingdom. This includes driving on the [left](https://goo.gl/maps/fcPxAUuyzEzRf9Ak7), as well as [directional signs](https://goo.gl/maps/kNsowiMi9Dd68QKt5), [chevrons](https://goo.gl/maps/NBkNUYfGXL1baFQr8) and [warning signs](https://goo.gl/maps/dK5pbVjsHtbSjTAo6). Some of the subtle differences are discussed below.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/jersey/French_locality_names.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/hiy6f3idNfjqVX4k9)

Due to its proximity to France, the vast majority of street and locality names contain **French** words. The island’s traditional language, known as [Jèrriais](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/J%C3%A8rriais), is closely related to French and appears on some [signs](https://goo.gl/maps/gfXPVEU3CehwnLD79).

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/jersey/Architecture.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/LcvutL9CdwxfhHj99)

Similarly, the local **architecture** shares many characteristics with that of northern France. This includes pastel-coloured walls and dark slate roofs. However, [British-style](https://goo.gl/maps/j6uEGrojSRmpRXPS8) terraced houses are also common.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/jersey/Red_granite.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/DnEYBXMfM4c1Ujtp9)

Jersey is home to large reserves of **red granite**. As a result, it is a popular building material used for the construction of many **walls** and houses throughout the island.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/jersey/Yellow_phone_booths.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/iFPqaRvG8CRTceZS8)

In contrast to the iconic red booths found on the mainland of the UK, **telephone booths** in Jersey are uniquely yellow.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/jersey/Cornish_palms.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/jQ4YQqArA5RTUqc58)

**Cornish palms** (Cordyline australis) can be identified by their distinctive thin trunks and arching leaves. They are commonly used as decorative plants on Jersey.

NOTE: These palms can also be found on some stretches of the British mainland’s coastline.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/jersey/Yellow_give_way_lines.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/vnVHzTAacVepY9Rq9)

Jersey uses **yellow give-way lines** at intersections.

NOTE: In contrast, the UK typically uses double dotted [white lines](https://goo.gl/maps/bKPN9mD7NQVUyoUi9) for this purpose.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/jersey/Cycling_route_signs.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/t9MpARHDYuihRv8d9)

Jersey has an extensive network of cycling routes. Most notably, you will find [signs](https://goo.gl/maps/i9MW5htcqMZYhqRq9) referencing the start or end of a **“Green Lane”**. Blue [**route markers**](https://goo.gl/maps/kqz88wFjRsujGHFx5), containing a map of the island in the centre, are also common.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/jersey/Signpost_attachments.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/rbihnHPMsp1vnZ6fA)

Some road signs are **asymmetrically** attached to their signposts. This appears to be more prevalent on Jersey than on the British mainland.

NOTE: Keep in mind that this only applies to a minority of signs. Most are attached regularly.

### Step 2 - Regional and parish-specific clues

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/jersey/Parish_Specific_Street_Name_Signs.png)](https://www.plonkit.net/images/jersey/Parish_Specific_Street_Name_Signs.png)

Most street name signs in Jersey are adorned with the **coat of arms** of the parish in which they are located.

*You can click on the graphic to enlarge it.*

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/jersey/St_Helier.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/BxaLSkap2xWNcBXy5)

Contrasting with the island’s typically rural look, the area surrounding the capital city of **St Helier** is more urbanised, featuring taller buildings, narrower streets and a more lively atmosphere.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/jersey/Les_Mielles.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/bp9FhjHHZ49obVyD9)

The Les Mielles Nature Reserve is a vast, **treeless** expanse located on Jersey’s western coast. Combined with the gentle westward slope and partially overcast coverage, this makes for a very recognisable area.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/jersey/October_glitch.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/6Tp99zJtzia6R1U99)

Imagery containing a greenish-purple **discolouration** can be found in the southwest of the island.

NOTE: This glitch typically makes parts of the screen appear darker than they actually are. In addition, it blends into the landscape more seamlessly than [other glitches](https://goo.gl/maps/4B8quhU7FXLricWn7).

Moreover, this glitch is not unique to Jersey. It can be found in other countries as well.

### Step 3 - Spotlight

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/jersey/Airport.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/uQCNqkpeyDq3qMdS6)

The roads surrounding **Jersey Airport** can be recognized thanks to the visible boundary **fence**.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/jersey/La_Collette_Power_Station.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/WeysZYdgsLZUX7ED8)

The La Collette Power Station is located on a peninsula south of the capital. Its **chimney** can be seen from various points along the south coast and serves as a useful reference point for pinpointing.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/jersey/Victoria_Ave.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/NJVoAHhYBzHgVNGw7)

**Victoria Ave** runs along the island’s southern coast, immediately west of St Helier. It is the only **divided** road on Jersey and features a distinct maroon median.
