### Step 1 - Identifying Thailand

![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/thailand/0_Copy_of_Copy_of_Copy_of_Copy_of_Copy_of_Copy_of_Copy_of_Infographic.png)

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/thailand/Thailand_License_Plate.png)](https://www.plonkit.net/images/thailand/Thailand_License_Plate.png)

Thailand uses large, **white** licence plates on **passenger vehicles**, and yellow ones on commercial vehicles.

NOTE: Within Southeast Asia, yellow commercial plates are only found in Thailand. Be careful, however, as Laos uses yellow licence plates for regular vehicles.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/thailand/Untitled_design_289_29.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/xU4Pi3hUfUAnHvSp6)

Thailand drives on the **left hand** side of the road.

NOTE: Cambodia and Laos drive on the right hand side.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/thailand/2022-12-22_41.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/WNbKinfDUShWZziU8)

The Thai bollard is square shaped with a pointed top, and has **black-and-white stripes**.

NOTE: In Southeast Asia, these bollards are also found in Cambodia, Laos and Indonesia.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/thailand/round.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/dzS5FHMz1koNS9z46)

Signposts in Thailand are square and bulky in shape, usually white, and often have the **bottom painted black**.

NOTE: In Southeast Asia, this design is unique to Thailand.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/thailand/th_concrete_roads.webp)](https://goo.gl/maps/UuCnGp7QtF9Sh6269)

**Concrete roads** are common in Thailand. They often have a large square shaped pattern.

NOTE: In Southeast Asia, these can also be found commonly in the Philippines, and less commonly in Indonesia.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/thailand/th_square_poles.webp)](https://goo.gl/maps/UkmmFgZewH869iht7)

Thai poles are **square** and have small **holes** running vertically up the pole.

NOTE: You will see similar poles in Sri Lanka, Cambodia and Laos. Make sure to look for other clues before guessing.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/thailand/th_language.png)](https://www.plonkit.net/images/thailand/th_language.png)

**Thai script** is compact and in many fonts has tiny circles within most letters.

NOTE: The Cambodian (Khmer) script often has longer lines and more accents underneath the lettering, as opposed to the Thai script. The Lao script looks similar to the Thai script. Make sure to check the driving side before guessing.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/thailand/2022-12-22_42.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/nZhMTSY41YTKfH3B6)

Thailand has **yellow middle lines**. These middle lines are either solid or [dashed](https://goo.gl/maps/3skrWJJNZ7JxFzyi7) lines.

NOTE: Malaysia only has white middle lines.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/thailand/Street_Light_Markings.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/oz7V87DcmcXSfoke8)

Street light poles have **red-and-black** or **black-and-white** stripes on the bottom.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/thailand/lamp.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/ejsjyNCE8BV7KpdNA)

Thailand has very distinct long, thin **lamp posts** that hang over the side of the road.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/thailand/Road_Number_Signs.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/fzFeKsgSovY2wJwK7)

Directional signs on smaller roads are **white**, and are composed of two signs: one with an arrow at the bottom, and one with the information at the top. These can have place names. When they do not, you will see them as just the road number with a [Garuda](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Emblem_of_Thailand). Moreover, highway signs are **green**, and frequently feature the place names in Latin script below the Thai name.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/thailand/Screenshot%20(638).png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/SfLXVkVXWeWw1cFh8)

Stop signs in Thailand feature the **Thai script**, instead of Latin.

NOTE: Stop signs in Laos feature the Lao script, similar to Thai, but they have just 2 characters instead of 3.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/thailand/Untitled_design8.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/HmhGFKoJU2daCxiw7)

These two road markers are generally found in Thailand. They will either have a white or a black **Garuda** on them.

NOTE: White Garudas are found on country roads and appear white on the map, while black ones are found on province roads and appear dark blue on the map.

[On the map, national roads are white-and-black, while provincial roads are blue-and-yellow.](https://www.plonkit.net/images/thailand/Thai+roads.png)

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/thailand/2022-12-24__281_29.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/3gdvYj8EPVopXjGc7)

Thailand often has many flags lining the side of its streets. Aside from the national flag, you can often see [royal flags](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Royal_flags_of_Thailand), which are usually **yellow**.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/thailand/greensign.png)](https://www.google.de/maps/@16.6874654,104.0909787,3a,31.5y,2.07h,84.05t/data=!3m6!1e1!3m4!1sILbeC_JPUdgreLQ2jSPoNw!2e0!7i16384!8i8192?shorturl=1)

These distinctive **green** signs can be found all around Thailand, often in small villages.

NOTE: You will not find this sign in any other country.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/thailand/gen3nocar.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/6RArTU8VoxKVnX1X7)

Most Generation 3 coverage has **no visible Street View car**.

NOTE: [Vientiane](https://maps.app.goo.gl/E53R9yBhmgzxiKaY8) and some Generation 3 coverage in [Cambodia](https://maps.app.goo.gl/G3n69goD22xopDMg7) will also not have a visible car. [Bike trekkers](https://maps.app.goo.gl/89XmAqRyVAqEU8wK6) and a [white car with a brake light](https://maps.app.goo.gl/SnHxSHmoqq64yXfr6) can rarely be found all throughout the country in Generation 3.

### Step 2 - Regional/province-specific clues

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/thailand/Indicatifs_Thailande.png)](https://www.plonkit.net/images/thailand/Indicatifs_Thailande.png)

Thai **landline phone numbers** have 9 digits. The first digits of phone numbers are the area code. The Bangkok area code is 02, the other area codes have three digits. The area codes are grouped, so even just learning the first digit is useful.

NOTE: Mobile phone numbers start with 08 or 09 and have 10 digits. None of these numbers overlap with the area codes. Numbers starting with 06 are also not regional.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/thailand/thailand_national_roads.png)](https://www.plonkit.net/images/thailand/thailand_national_roads.png)

National roads are grouped into four regions based on their **first digit**. Roads beginning with 1 can be found in the north, 2 in the northeast, 3 in the centre and 4 in the south of the country.

NOTE: Large one- and two-digit highways may pass through multiple regions. Remember that this map does not apply to provincial roads.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/thailand/abbreviations.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/XbrkT9597arMirCw7)

Waystones and road signs on provincial roads will often provide a **two-letter abbreviation** of the province in Thai script. They will not appear on waystones or road signs for national roads.

You can use [this](https://super-duper.fr/country/thailand_provinces_en.php) site to view and practice all province abbreviations.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/thailand/blackboxes.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/J4jH4yMEG7r3WFPa9)

These **black boxes** will often provide a **three-letter abbreviation** of the province. Two formats are used:

- Starting with the abbreviation followed by 4 numbers
- Starting with TCC-L2, or rarely TCC-L1, followed by the abbreviation

Shown in the picture is the abbreviation “NTB” for Nonthaburi. The abbreviations may vary slightly in each province depending on the format.

NOTE: Other boxes may feature different formats that correspond to the subdistrict of the province.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/thailand/islam.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/5QCCKXbXDJzc2SPm9)

The majority of **Muslims** in Thailand live in the very south of the country. In these areas, you will often see Islamic religious attire, [Jawi script](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jawi_script), based on the Arabic script, on [signs](https://maps.app.goo.gl/oy83kvBm8XWcc3ZEA) and mosques.

#### Landscapes

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/thailand/Landscape_-_Eastern_Flat_and_Dry.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/G677huTmDgZuNQj68)

Thailand has two regions that are relatively **flat and dry** compared to other areas. One north of Bangkok, the other more east.

NOTE: The eastern area feels sometimes a bit **drier** than the area north of Bangkok.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/thailand/Landscape_-_Tall_Mountains_Northwest.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/2F4kZSQecS6C4igH7)

**Tall mountains** are most often seen in northwesternThailand.

NOTE: Some parts of the southern peninsular part are also mountainous.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/thailand/lowmountains.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/5oTu6naatCLJEZZr5)

Low, smooth and forested mountains can be found in the centre-north of the country.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/thailand/exposedrockmountain.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/8GvEz2TG2jPQjjPJ9)

Steep mountains with a lot of **exposed rock** are most commonly found in the west and on the peninsula.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/thailand/redsoil.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/sMZDe9xwADPqCgsS6)

**Red soil** is most common in the northeast of Thailand, but can be seen throughout the entire country.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/thailand/gravelroad.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/JkTMQ3oUA7i6ATvd8)

**Gravel roads** can be found all throughout Thailand, but are most common in the darkly highlighted areas.

#### Agriculture and vegetation

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/thailand/Rubber_Wood_Plantations.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/UNsgYPDDnM7BtfBD6)

**Rubber wood plantations** are typically seen in Thailand. Most of them are located in thesouth. You can recognize them by the small, black taps on the bottom of the tree's trunk.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/thailand/th_rice.png)](https://www.google.com/maps/@15.7410186,103.7087319,3a,75y,167.97h,69.52t/data=!3m6!1e1!3m4!1sV7uZg8thTdZnjTXF-h0NwA!2e0!7i16384!8i8192)

A majority of Thailand’s **rice** cultivation occurs within the highlighted area.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/thailand/dryseasonrice.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/XhZdHEjsXAE77Q5n7)

**Rice** cultivated during the **dry season** is most common in the central plains. The dry season in Thailand can typically be recognized by a haze in the sky due to wildfires.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/thailand/wetseasonrice.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/3fVXtxRKPcnbnfq89)

During the **wet season**, the majority of **rice** cultivation occurs in the eastern plains.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/thailand/zsNKCmO.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/sKXbSH497oxfgLvq7)

Over 90% of the palm oil in Thailand is produced in the south. This means that **palm plantations** are most often seen in southern Thailand.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/thailand/shortcoconutplantation.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/wLzZoMFpsEfWZ8xa7)

Dense plantations of **short** or **medium sized coconut palms** are most commonly found westof Bangkok.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/thailand/tallcoconutplantation.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/TQEraC5cNeqTEPgTA)

Slightly less dense plantations of **tall coconut palms** are most common in Prachuap Khiri Khan.

NOTE: The entire peninsula will have many coconut palms, this tip only refers to plantations.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/thailand/palmyrapalms.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/NZNdXrpYZk5fV6Ji8)

Large numbers of **palmyra palms** are typically found near the eastern coast of the peninsula, specifically just south of Bangkok as well as in the far south.

NOTE: Lone or small clusters of palmyra palms can be found all throughout the country.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/thailand/arecapalms.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/ema3ML7yqzroNTzeA)

**Areca palms** are mainly found across the peninsula, but are especially common around Ranong.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/thailand/sugarcane.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/442E3pgwVVHWdxCs8)

**Sugar cane** is mostly grown in central and eastern Thailand.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/thailand/cassava.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/HVkRqVgCXCAsabet8)

[**Cassava**](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cassava) is a bush-like plant with [digitate leaves](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Glossary_of_leaf_morphology#/media/File:Leaf_morphology.svg). Itcan be found throughout most of Thailand, but is considerably more common in the central and northeastern plains.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/thailand/7xwo2Rl.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/mtmCPEvpmE6kjDJW8)

**Corn** is most commonly found in the north and centralregions of Thailand.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/thailand/jDPRtU0.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/CryJsAwwvsCratiWA)

The most extensive **fruit plantations** can be found in the valley between Chiang Mai and Lamphunas well as eastern Sa Kaeo and Chanthaburi.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/thailand/pk9UXvb.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/HemvSzRtg8KbUriK8)

**Durian plantations** are most common around Chanthaburi, but can be found around the peninsula and other areas. When they are young, they can be identified by their cone-shaped appearance. When [fully grown](https://maps.app.goo.gl/3TtjWxAX8eC8m72f7), durian trees can be identified by their dark bark, branches that nearly reach the ground, and oblong leaves with pointed tips.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/thailand/pineapple.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/rHzaooZmHgJhtBZy9)

**Pineapple plantations** are most commonly found southeast and southwestof Bangkok.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/thailand/uEC2dBG.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/PmZx1apdRvUV92578)

**Eucalyptus plantations** are most common around Sa Kaeo.

NOTE: Eucalyptus can be found in smaller quantities, such as lining the road, in different areas. This tip is exclusively about dense plantations.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/thailand/teak.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/iDwPvMFDXKPqzyuE9)

[Tectona grandis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Teak), also known as **teak**, is most commonly found in high densities in the north. It can be recognized by its large leaves and yellow flowers.

NOTE: Teak trees are often used for decoration along [highways](https://maps.app.goo.gl/8ZVapgFjkxUqz6UB8).

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/thailand/pinetrees.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/rZY8KhAicsnrWS2E9)

**Pine trees**, specifically [Pinus latteri](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pinus_latteri), or Tenasserim pines, are more or less exclusive to the north and northwest of the country.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/thailand/xeufVFg.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/WvVCRDEcKzCQ4ALb9)

[Codiaeum variegatum](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Codiaeum_variegatum), also known as **garden croton**, is a decorative plant recognised by its yellow, orange and red leaves. It is most common around Ranong and other coastal provinces.

#### Infrastructure

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/thailand/indentedpole.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/8V8yJ6NPqRYaeAJh8)

Regular poles with a Chilean style **indent** are found around Bangkok.

NOTE: Larger and thicker poles with indents are found on [highways](https://maps.app.goo.gl/8tzw2wz1WYcVC83R8) all throughout the country.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/thailand/windturbines.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/voFy633WGX9u84K78)

**Wind turbines** are most often found on the western side of Chaiyaphum and Nakhon Ratchasima as well as the eastern coast of Songkhla and Nakhon Si Thammarat.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/thailand/specialbollard.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/DLEg3FvRp8VxWce37)

This **black bollard** with **three large white reflectors** is most frequently seen in Chiang Mai and Mae Hong Son.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/thailand/7ridgeinsulator.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/kYR5V2U9de5vnrgk7)

Insulators with **7 or more ridges** are found in the southern half of the peninsula, as well as highways in Chiang Rai and Phayao.

NOTE: In the north, you may still find 5 or 6 ridges on insulators, as these are the standard amount throughout the country.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/thailand/oneinsulator.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/yGS12y3v7tbDKh4Q9)

Poles with only **one insulator** on the crossbar can be found from south on the peninsula to Ranong and Chumphon.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/thailand/twoinsulators.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/os4n3QswoPf28oLD9)

Conversely, poles with **two insulators** on the crossbar can be found in the rest of the country.

### Step 3 - Spotlight

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/thailand/phuket.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/6GAQihX6wAgnDAXM7)

The island of **Phuket** is very touristy and wealthy. You will often see modern buildings, resorts, and heavy traffic among hills.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/thailand/kohsamui.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/ANJ9uWAxtdssaSyW6)

Koh Samui and Koh Pha-Ngan are touristy islands with **narrow, crowded roads** and a tropical feel.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/thailand/bangkoktaxi.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/zBmTCi5N4ETLBumU9)

Bangkok uses **pink** and **yellow on green taxis**.

NOTE: Yellow on green taxis may rarely be seen [elsewhere](https://maps.app.goo.gl/Jf25hEoRKt9tQhvEA), but pink taxis are practically exclusive.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/thailand/chiangmairedtruck.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/ydrtb1Hsc9SVKueBA)

These red trucks, known as **songthaews**, are transport vehicles found most commonly in Chiang Mai.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/thailand/bangkokstreetsign.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/4VF9cTHGw4u5y6u19)

Street signs with a **smaller** **green section** underneath a blue section are found in Bangkok and Nonthaburi.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/thailand/pattayastreetsign.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/7SribRKbntJ3Rbb56)

Street signs shaped like a **helm** are exclusive to Pattaya City.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/thailand/chiangraistreetsign.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/2tzKiZxZCTWyJ4xm8)

**Red street signs** with golden decorated borders can be found in Mueang Chiang Rai.

NOTE: It is possible to see blue street signs with the same border in the city.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/thailand/phraovalley.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/8yrmgij7ZSn6KyVQ7)

The **Phrao District** in Chiang Mai is a fairly flat valley surrounded by mountains in all directions. The valley will commonly grow rice and fruit.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/thailand/doiluangchiangdao.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/V95hWp5BDFpr2AHu9)

**Doi Luang Chiang Dao** is the 3rd tallest mountain in Thailand. It along with other distinctly shaped mountains can be seen clearly from the Chiang Dao district of Chiang Mai.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/thailand/phayaomountains.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/UunDzfgwCXFs16Wm8)

A large and slightly jagged north-south mountain ridge runs through the middle of **Phayao**. It can be seen to the east from afar.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/thailand/westerntak.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/5vnLtPy476ekhvrR6)

**Rolling hills** of corn or sugar cane fields can be found in the west of the Tak province. In this area, paved roads are often [very brown](https://maps.app.goo.gl/gFH71bddWWbfX9Pz5).

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/thailand/FFCwiY9.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/tWNmWVA3EDbHxHpq9)

**Large north-south mountains** can be seen on either side of the Phetchabun valley.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/thailand/kanchanaburifields.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/wha9uFUotrBMY5vJ8)

**Deep red fields** with cassava and rubber trees can be found in Kanchanaburi.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/thailand/lopburimountains.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/z5FGU3sPwMZDzmkSA)

These distinctly shaped mountains can be found in **southwestern Lopburi**.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/thailand/saraburimountains.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/qwg5wBjmYh9asKAP8)

The northern half of Saraburi consists of **large, exposed rock mountains** in an east-west orientation.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/thailand/chaiyaphumridges.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/ftNe5yJv6jg2JiE57)

Chaiyaphum features **very long and flat mountain ridges** that can be seen from nearly anywhere in the province.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/thailand/khaoyaiNP.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/ee9x9HVyPLAS1dbQA)

The area around Khao Yai National Park in southern Nakhon Ratchasima is a **large tourist attraction**. You will see many advertisements, businesses, restaurants, and resorts along otherwise rural roads. Large rocky hills can be seen closer to the border with Nakhon Nayok and Prachin Buri.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/thailand/mukdahanhills.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/mEN45TfKSd9FBhD77)

The province of Mukdahan features many **long, low hills** among fields of rice, sugarcane, cassava and rubber trees.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/thailand/khaosamroiyotNP.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/sL5PdxX9pBvi7XZ86)

Khao Sam Roi Yot National Park in western Prachuap Khiri Khan features large, distinct exposed rock mountain massifs. Notably, the mountains can be seen from the [center of the province](https://maps.app.goo.gl/SNn2WAdVCjAdX8mf9).

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/thailand/khaosokNP.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/JF6QD3NQtGJbeFbH6)

Khao Sok National Park in northern Phang-nga and western Surat Thani features some of the **tallest and steepest exposed rock mountains** in the south of Thailand. The mountains generally run east-west.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/thailand/westernkrabimountains.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/xfHxntSbxjMBsai78)

Western Krabi and parts of eastern Phang-nga also feature **large exposed rock mountains** running north-south.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/thailand/nakhonratchasimaSE.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/TGZwr7DBSQr6o3gSA)

**Brown paved roads** among fields of sugarcane, cassava, and corn can be found in southeastern Nakhon Ratchasima.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/thailand/southsisaket.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/VS75Aa3JYD4BC4fd6)

**Brown paved roads** among fields of bananas, durians, rubber, and corn can be found in southern Sisaket.

NOTE: Southeastern Nakhon Ratchasima may look [similar](https://maps.app.goo.gl/3px9g5rxuA6NbzzL8) at times.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/thailand/lopburigrid.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/7xEjHmJF1WTYkkk86)

Southern Lopburi features a large, slightly **north-north-west grid** with fields of rice, corn, sugarcane, and cassava. Ridges can often be seen to the north, south and west.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/thailand/pathumthanigrid.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/iqZ9g6To6PavSkKH7)

A large, almost perfectly **north-south** **grid** can be found in central and eastern Pathum Thani.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/thailand/rayonggrid.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/2Y5eNnZEhT8i5iGN8)

Western Rayong province features a **northeast-southwest grid** filled with modern and industrial buildings among fields of rubber trees, cassava, and pineapple.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/thailand/hwy44.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/FvDCNBvgA8bWMfqW9)

Highway 44 going through Krabi and Surat Thani is a **divided four-lane highway** with **very large spacing** in the middle. Often, you will not be able to see the other side of the highway. Rather, a yellow inner line will imply that the road is divided.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/thailand/jOVfr8B.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/m4sGpjw5wNW4WxZZ7)

This rare Google car with a **visible mirror** is only found in far southern Thailand.

NOTE: Due to most of the coverage being overwritten by new Generation 4, this car is rarely seen.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/thailand/Ko_Sichang_Car.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/7V7ec8nvg4JG3Wx67)

This rare Google car is only found on the island of Ko Sichang. It is recognizable due to the **bar(s) protruding out of the blur**.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/thailand/khaoyaismudge.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/Gxwah4w61mKW1rj97)

A **smudge** on the **front left** of the camera can be seen on the densely forested road going through Khao Yai National Park. This smudge can also be found in nearby areas of Nakhon Nayok and Prachin Buri.

NOTE: Other smudges may rarely exist [elsewhere](https://maps.app.goo.gl/GYwj41y21n9awFLT6) in the country.
