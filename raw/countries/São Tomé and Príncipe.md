### Step 1 - Identifying São Tomé & Príncipe

![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/sao-tome-and-principe/0_st_summary.png)

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/sao-tome-and-principe/st_shit.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/LTkTMgjD7rxFCcgE8)

All the coverage was taken with a low quality camera that’s commonly referred to as “Shitcam”. The car will often be completely blurred, with a **very large blur** covering the back and a [somewhat smaller blur](https://goo.gl/maps/YfaEf11wuzdt3NYv8) covering the front.

NOTE: The coverage is a newer variant of shitcam, where the image is dimmer and hazier than the original shitcam primarily found in [India](https://maps.app.goo.gl/VPnHTFZzAzte9pWP6) and [Nigeria](https://maps.app.goo.gl/eETqUgMHvyiCR4di8).

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/sao-tome-and-principe/st_language.png)](https://www.plonkit.net/images/sao-tome-and-principe/st_language.png)

**Portuguese** is the main language used on São Tomé and Príncipe.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/sao-tome-and-principe/st_polepedestriancrossing.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/4ojwFdj6pSKUkbeF8)

On top of the language, the Portuguese influence can also be seen in infrastructure, such as ladder poles and pedestrian signs with the same design as in Portugal.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/sao-tome-and-principe/st_tropical.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/qjvx6rFCZPN7M3QdA)

São Tomé and Príncipe are **tropical islands**, which are almost completely covered in rainforests. Banana plants are especially common throughout the islands.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/sao-tome-and-principe/antenna.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/4VeKVBatyNNenp8y9)

In many locations, you will notice a **slanted antenna** protruding from the side of the car.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/sao-tome-and-principe/st_bluepolepaint.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/6x7TkiEeH4v4XSGL7)

Some wooden poles are painted **blue** on the bottom. You can also read the letters **CST** on them.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/sao-tome-and-principe/yellow_taxi_sao_tome.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/JkNYuG5P1kwrHWMa8)

Taxis and minibuses are painted in a **bright yellow** colour.

### Step 2 - Region-specific clues

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/sao-tome-and-principe/st_principecam.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/rMu2eGbM2tD2KF719)

This desaturated and even dimmer variant of shitcam is most common on Príncipe.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/sao-tome-and-principe/principe_car.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/gFGYZg3wL1LP4acT9)

The car used on Príncipe can be recognised by the **blue** roof and silver roof rails.

NOTE: Remember that it can sometimes be blurred out.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/sao-tome-and-principe/sao_tome_car.png)](https://maps.app.goo.gl/XNmPyAVxnjhpm81n7)

The car used on São Tomé is **black** with black roof rails. In most locations, it is blurred out.

### Step 3 - Spotlight

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/sao-tome-and-principe/st_oilpalmplantation.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/13XwZpCMeKqxn6wG7)

You can find **oil palm plantations** in southern São Tomé.

[![](https://www.plonkit.net/images/resize/1920/80/sao-tome-and-principe/st_trinidadpolepaint.png)](https://goo.gl/maps/1Aty7JQDMLYERf7N8)

The town of Trinidade has poles with **red and white** pole paint.
